Masjid Al-Aqsa: History, Sanctity, and Responsibility

Al-Aqsa is the first qiblah, the second masjid on earth, and the land of Isra and Mi'raj. This article traces its Qur'anic roots, prophetic narrations, early Muslim stewardship, and why safeguarding its sanctity remains a duty for the global ummah today.

Key Takeaways

  1. Masjid Al-Aqsa is blessed in Qur'an 17:1 and was the qiblah for early Muslims; its sanctity is tied to Tawhid and prophetic history.
  2. Worship in Al-Aqsa carries multiplied reward and connects believers to the legacy of many prophets who prayed there.
  3. Safeguarding Al-Aqsa demands dua, education, advocacy, and ethical support for those protecting its sanctity.
Rate this summary:

Introduction

Al-Aqsa anchors Muslim memory: it hosted the Isra and Mi'raj, was the first direction of prayer, and remains a sign of prophetic continuity. The Qur'an calls its surroundings blessed, reminding every generation to protect and honor it.

Qur'anic and Prophetic Basis

Surah Al-Isra (17:1) narrates the Prophet's night journey to Al-Masjid Al-Aqsa, linking Mecca and Jerusalem. Numerous hadith mention its virtue, including the encouragement to visit the three sacred mosques (Bukhari, Muslim). The Prophet ? led all prophets in prayer there, symbolizing leadership and unity.

Early History and Liberation

Built after Al-Masjid Al-Haram by forty years (hadith), Al-Aqsa witnessed the rule of prophets and righteous kings. Umar ibn Al-Khattab restored and safeguarded it with justice after Jerusalem's peaceful entry, establishing protections for its people and sanctuaries.

Virtues of Worship in Al-Aqsa

Prayer in Al-Aqsa is worth 250 prayers (according to several narrations). It is one of the three mosques to which travel for worship is recommended. Visiting ties the believer to a chain of devotion stretching from earlier prophets to the Ummah of Muhammad ?.

Stewardship and Preservation

Islamic stewardship includes protecting its endowments (awqaf), maintaining scholarship on its virtues, and ensuring pilgrims practice adab: modesty, cleanliness, and reverence. Supporting restoration and education preserves its status for future generations.

Contemporary Challenges

Political instability, restrictions on worshippers, and misinformation threaten Al-Aqsa's sanctity. Disinformation often blurs the distinction between Al-Aqsa, the surrounding precinct, and the Dome of the Rock. Clear teaching and credible sources are essential.

What the Ummah Can Do

Engage in dua for its people, support reputable relief and legal aid organizations, learn and teach its history, and correct misconceptions respectfully. Visiting when safe, amplifying authentic scholarship, and advocating for protected access are meaningful contributions.

Conclusion

Al-Aqsa embodies Tawhid, prophecy, and communal responsibility. Honoring it with knowledge, dua, and advocacy is part of honoring the Prophet ? and his legacy.

Notes

  1. Qur'an 17:1 on Isra to Al-Masjid Al-Aqsa.
  2. Hadith on visiting three mosques (Bukhari, Muslim).
  3. Narrations on the reward of prayer in Al-Aqsa and its construction forty years after Al-Haram.
