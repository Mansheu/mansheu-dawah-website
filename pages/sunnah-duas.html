The Sunnah duas are authentic supplications directly taught by Prophet Muhammad ﷺ through his sayings, actions, and approvals. These duas have been preserved in the collections of hadith and represent the perfect way to communicate with Allah in various life situations.

The Prophet ﷺ said: "Nothing is more honored by Allah than supplication." (Tirmidhi 3370) These prophetic duas cover all aspects of daily life - from seeking guidance and protection to expressing gratitude and seeking forgiveness.

Following the Prophet's ﷺ example in supplication brings us closer to his blessed way of life and ensures our duas are in accordance with Islamic teachings and proper etiquette.

Dua for Guidance in Difficulty (Istikharah)

اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْتَخِيرُكَ بِعِلْمِكَ، وَأَسْتَقْدِرُكَ بِقُدْرَتِكَ، وَأَسْأَلُكَ مِنْ فَضْلِكَ الْعَظِيمِ، فَإِنَّكَ تَقْدِرُ وَلَا أَقْدِرُ، وَتَعْلَمُ وَلَا أَعْلَمُ، وَأَنْتَ عَلَّامُ الْغُيُوبِ، اللَّهُمَّ إِنْ كُنْتَ تَعْلَمُ أَنَّ هَذَا الْأَمْرَ خَيْرٌ لِي فِي دِينِي وَمَعَاشِي وَعَاقِبَةِ أَمْرِي فَاقْدُرْهُ لِي وَيَسِّرْهُ لِي ثُمَّ بَارِكْ لِي فِيهِ، وَإِنْ كُنْتَ تَعْلَمُ أَنَّ هَذَا الْأَمْرَ شَرٌّ لِي فِي دِينِي وَمَعَاشِي وَعَاقِبَةِ أَمْرِي فَاصْرِفْهُ عَنِّي وَاصْرِفْنِي عَنْهُ، وَاقْدُرْ لِيَ الْخَيْرَ حَيْثُ كَانَ ثُمَّ أَرْضِنِي بِهِ

(When seeking Allah's guidance in making decisions)

O Allah, I seek Your guidance by virtue of Your knowledge, and I seek ability by virtue of Your power, and I ask You of Your great bounty. You have power, I have none. And You know, I know not. You are the Knower of hidden things. O Allah, if You know that this matter is good for me in my religion, my livelihood and my affairs, both immediate and in the future, then ordain it for me, make it easy for me, and bless it for me. And if You know that this matter is bad for me in my religion, my livelihood and my affairs, both immediate and in the future, then turn it away from me, and turn me away from it. And ordain for me the good wherever it may be, and make me content with it.

Allāhumma innī astakhīruka bi-ʿilmik, wa astaqdiruka bi-qudratik, wa as'aluka min faḍlika al-ʿaẓīm, fa-innaka taqdiru wa lā aqdir, wa taʿlamu wa lā aʿlam, wa anta ʿallāmu al-ghuyūb. Allāhumma in kunta taʿlamu anna hādhā al-amra khayrun lī fī dīnī wa maʿāshī wa ʿāqibati amrī fa-qdurhu lī wa yassirhu lī thumma bārik lī fīh. Wa in kunta taʿlamu anna hādhā al-amra sharrun lī fī dīnī wa maʿāshī wa ʿāqibati amrī fa-ṣrifhu ʿannī wa-ṣrifnī ʿanhu, wa-qdur liya al-khayra ḥaythu kān thumma arḍinī bih.

Direct Teaching from the Prophet ﷺ

Jābir ibn ʿAbdullāh رضي الله عنه said: "The Messenger of Allah ﷺ used to teach us istikharah for all matters, just as he would teach us a chapter from the Quran." (Bukhārī 1162)

Prevents Regret and Wrong Choices

The Prophet ﷺ said: "Whoever performs istikharah will not regret, and whoever consults others will not be blamed." (Ḥākim, authenticated by Albānī)

Complete Submission to Allah's Will

This dua represents perfect submission to Allah's wisdom and knowledge, acknowledging our limitations while seeking His infinite guidance in all affairs of life.

Seeking Allah's Knowledge

• Acknowledges Allah's infinite knowledge vs our limited understanding

• Recognizes Allah as the "Knower of the Unseen" (ʿAllāmu al-Ghuyūb)

• Shows humility in decision-making

Three-Fold Impact Assessment

• Religion (Dīn) - spiritual and moral consequences

• Livelihood (Maʿāsh) - worldly and material effects

• Future outcome (ʿĀqibah) - long-term results

Balanced Request

• If good: ordain it, make it easy, and bless it

• If bad: turn it away from me and turn me away from it

• Shows trust in Allah's perfect wisdom

How to Perform Istikharah

• Perform two units of voluntary prayer first

• Recite this dua with sincere intention

• Mention the specific matter you're deciding about

• Trust Allah's guidance through subsequent events and feelings

Comprehensive Protection Dua

اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنَ الْهَمِّ وَالْحَزَنِ، وَأَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنَ الْعَجْزِ وَالْكَسَلِ، وَأَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنَ الْجُبْنِ وَالْبُخْلِ، وَأَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ غَلَبَةِ الدَّيْنِ وَقَهْرِ الرِّجَالِ

(Frequently recited by the Prophet ﷺ)

O Allah, I seek refuge in You from anxiety and sorrow, weakness and laziness, miserliness and cowardice, the burden of debts and from being overpowered by men.

Allāhumma innī aʿūdhu bika min al-hammi wa al-ḥazan, wa aʿūdhu bika min al-ʿajzi wa al-kasal, wa aʿūdhu bika min al-jubni wa al-bukhl, wa aʿūdhu bika min ghalabati ad-dayni wa qahri ar-rijāl.

Most Frequent Dua of the Prophet ﷺ

Anas ibn Mālik رضي الله عنه reported: "The most frequent dua of the Prophet ﷺ was this dua." (Bukhārī 2893, Muslim 2706)

Comprehensive Protection

This dua covers all major psychological, physical, and social challenges that can affect a Muslim's spiritual and worldly life, providing complete protection through Allah's refuge.

Addressing Root Causes

The Prophet ﷺ identified these as the primary sources of human weakness and difficulty, making this dua essential for maintaining spiritual strength and worldly success.

Emotional Protection

• Hamm - Anxiety about future events

• Ḥazan - Sorrow over past events

• Together, these cover all temporal emotional distress

Character Weaknesses

• ʿAjz - Weakness, inability to fulfill duties

• Kasal - Laziness, procrastination in good deeds

• Both prevent spiritual and worldly achievement

Social Character Flaws

• Jubn - Cowardice in standing for truth

• Bukhl - Miserliness, withholding what should be given

• These damage relationships and social responsibility

External Pressures

• Ghalabat ad-dayn - Overwhelming debt

• Qahr ar-rijāl - Being dominated by people

• Protection from financial and social oppression

Dua for Relief from Distress

لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ الْعَظِيمُ الْحَلِيمُ، لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ رَبُّ الْعَرْشِ الْعَظِيمِ، لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ رَبُّ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَرَبُّ الْأَرْضِ وَرَبُّ الْعَرْشِ الْكَرِيمِ

(When facing distress or hardship)

There is no deity except Allah, the Magnificent, the Forbearing. There is no deity except Allah, Lord of the Magnificent Throne. There is no deity except Allah, Lord of the heavens, Lord of the earth, and Lord of the Noble Throne.

Lā ilāha illa-llāhu al-ʿAẓīmu al-Ḥalīm, lā ilāha illa-llāhu Rabbu al-ʿArshi al-ʿAẓīm, lā ilāha illa-llāhu Rabbu as-samāwāti wa Rabbu al-arḍi wa Rabbu al-ʿArshi al-Karīm.

Prophet's ﷺ Dua During Distress

Ibn ʿAbbās رضي الله عنهما reported: "When the Prophet ﷺ was afflicted with distress, he would say this dua." (Bukhārī 6345, Muslim 2730)

Immediate Relief

This dua brings immediate comfort to the heart by reminding us of Allah's absolute control, His gentleness with His servants, and His supreme authority over all creation.

Triple Declaration of Tawhid

The threefold repetition of "Lā ilāha illa-llāh" with different attributes creates a powerful spiritual impact, strengthening faith and dissolving anxiety through recognition of Allah's sovereignty.

Allah's Magnificent Attributes

• Al-ʿAẓīm - The Magnificent, infinitely great

• Al-Ḥalīm - The Forbearing, patient with His servants

• Combines Allah's majesty with His mercy

Lord of the Throne

• The Throne (ʿArsh) represents Allah's supreme authority

• Described as both "Great" (ʿAẓīm) and "Noble" (Karīm)

• Shows Allah's complete control over all affairs

Universal Lordship

• "Rabbu as-samāwāti" - Lord of the heavens

• "Rabbu al-arḍ" - Lord of the earth

• Nothing in creation is outside Allah's dominion

Therapeutic Effect

• Redirects focus from problems to Allah's power

• Reminds that no difficulty is beyond Allah's ability to resolve

• Creates tranquility through acknowledgment of divine sovereignty

• Strengthens trust in Allah's wisdom and timing
