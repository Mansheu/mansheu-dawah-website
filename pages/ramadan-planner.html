About This Printable

The Ramadan Planner was built by our Resource Team after years of community workshops. Each page guides you through intentional mornings, mindful afternoons, and spiritually rich nights so that no day of the blessed month passes without purpose.

You will find daily spreads for salah checklists, Qur'an pacing, hydration reminders, suhoor and iftar menus, sadaqah ideas, dua lists, and evening reflections. Special dashboards cover the last ten nights, Laylat al-Qadr prep, and Eid readiness so your family glides from Ramadan into celebration with ease.

The planner prints beautifully in colour with gentle teal accents, yet remains clear in greyscale for economical bulk printing in masjid classes or youth groups.

What's Included

  • 30 identical daily planning spreads with salah, Qur'an, dua, gratitude, and mood trackers
  • Weekly meal planner, grocery list, and leftovers log to reduce waste
  • Charity tracker for zakat, sadaqah jars, and volunteering hours
  • Last ten nights action plan with Qiyam schedule and Laylat al-Qadr checklist
  • Eid preparation section covering gifts, outfits, and visiting routes
  • Bonus page with inspirational duas and Qur'an verses for motivation

Suggested Uses

Home Base

Keep the planner on your kitchen counter so the whole family can add notes after suhoor.

Mosque Circles

Distribute to sisters' halaqat or youth taraweeh groups to encourage intentional worship.

Student Life

University students can balance exams and ibadah by pencilling in study blocks and rest.

Kids' Challenge

Invite tweens to fill their own pages with goals, good deeds, and favourite duas.

Work Routine

Schedule lunch-break Qur'an sessions, charity deductions, and after-work masjid visits.

Gift Bundle

Print, bind, and include in Ramadan care packages for loved ones or revert support circles.

Printing Tips

Planner-Friendly Printing

  • Print duplex (double-sided) to save paper and keep weekly spreads together.
  • Use 120gsm paper for durability when writing with markers; 90gsm works for everyday pens.
  • Bind with a simple comb, staple, or slip into a ring binder so pages lay flat.
  • Laminate reusable sections like meal plans; write with wet-erase markers.
  • Choose the black-and-white version when printing for classrooms or masjid giveaways.

How to Use

  1. Print the planner before Ramadan begins and note key dates (moon sighting events, community iftars).
  2. Each evening, outline the next day's prayers, Qur'an goal, meals, and sadaqah plan.
  3. Use the reflections space after taraweeh to jot gratitude, dua answers, or lessons learned.
  4. Update the charity tracker weekly to remain on pace for zakat or giving targets.
  5. Switch to the final section when the last ten nights arrive and circle Laylat al-Qadr strategies.
  6. Complete the Eid checklist three days before Ramadan ends to avoid last-minute stress.
