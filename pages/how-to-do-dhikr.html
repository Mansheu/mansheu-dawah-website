How to Do Dhikr

9 min read

A Practical Guide to the Remembrance of Allah

Dhikr is one of the most accessible acts of worship in Islam - it requires no preparation, no specific place, and can be done in any state. However, to maximize its benefits and rewards, there are certain methods, etiquettes, and best practices that the Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ) taught us. This guide will help you perform dhikr in the most beneficial way.

The Basic Requirements

Unlike prayer (salah), dhikr has minimal requirements:

  • No wudu required: You can do dhikr without ablution
  • No specific direction: You don't need to face the Qibla
  • Any time, any place: Dhikr can be done anywhere, anytime
  • In any state: Standing, sitting, lying down, walking, working

The Proper Methods

1. With the Tongue (Verbal Dhikr)

This is the most common form. You say the words of dhikr out loud or quietly. The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "The one who remembers his Lord and the one who does not remember his Lord are like the living and the dead."

Best Practice: Say the words clearly, even if quietly. Moving your lips engages both your tongue and your mind.

2. With the Heart (Silent Dhikr)

You can also do dhikr silently in your heart, especially when verbal dhikr is not possible (like during meetings or conversations). The heart's dhikr is when you consciously think of Allah and His attributes.

Important Note: Heart dhikr is valid, but the Prophet (ﷺ) generally practiced verbal dhikr because it combines both tongue and heart.

3. Through Actions (Practical Dhikr)

Acts of worship like prayer, charity, and good deeds are also forms of dhikr. When you pray with concentration, you're remembering Allah with your entire body.

Should You Count?

The Prophet (ﷺ) taught specific numbers for certain adhkar. There are three methods of counting:

Using Your Fingers

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "Count on your fingers, for they will be questioned and made to speak." (Tirmidhi)

This is the most recommended method. Use your right hand, and count with your thumb on your finger segments.

Using Prayer Beads (Tasbih/Misbaha)

While some scholars discourage this as it wasn't used by the early Muslims, many scholars permit it as a helpful tool, especially for those who find it difficult to concentrate or count on fingers. The tool itself is permissible; what matters is the intention and not showing off.

Digital Counters

In our modern age, digital tasbih counters and apps are available. They're permissible as tools, but remember: the Prophet's method of using fingers is the most blessed.

Loud or Silent?

Both are permissible, and each has its benefits:

Silent Dhikr:

  • More sincere and less likely to show off
  • Suitable when others are around
  • Good for maintaining continuous dhikr

Audible Dhikr:

  • Helps maintain focus and concentration
  • Can inspire others to remember Allah
  • Engages both tongue and heart
  • The Prophet (ﷺ) often did dhikr audibly

Golden Rule: Don't be so loud that you disturb others, and don't be completely silent if speaking helps your concentration. Find a balance.

The Essential Etiquettes (Adab) of Dhikr

1. Sincerity (Ikhlas)

Do dhikr purely for Allah's sake, not to show others or gain their praise. The Prophet (ﷺ) warned against showing off in worship.

2. Presence of Heart (Khushu)

Don't just move your tongue mechanically. Understand and reflect on what you're saying. When you say "SubhanAllah," think about Allah's perfection. When you say "Alhamdulillah," reflect on His blessings.

3. Consistency Over Quantity

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "The most beloved deeds to Allah are those that are most consistent, even if they are small." (Bukhari)

Better to say 10 adhkar every day with focus than 1000 once a month without concentration.

4. Use Authentic Adhkar

Stick to the adhkar taught by the Prophet (ﷺ). Don't invent new formulas. The Sunnah adhkar are the most blessed and guaranteed to be accepted.

5. Cleanliness is Preferred (Not Required)

While wudu is not required for dhikr, it's better to be in a state of purity when remembering Allah, if possible.

When to Do Dhikr: The Best Times

  1. After Fajr (Dawn Prayer): Until sunrise - highly rewarded time
  2. After Asr (Afternoon Prayer): Until sunset
  3. Before Sleeping: Protects you during sleep
  4. Upon Waking: First thing you do
  5. After Each Obligatory Prayer: Multiply your rewards
  6. During Commute: While driving, walking, on the bus
  7. While Waiting: In lines, waiting for appointments
  8. During Work Breaks: Instead of scrolling on your phone
  9. Last Third of the Night: If you wake up
  10. Friday: The blessed day of the week

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Rushing Through: Going so fast that you don't pronounce words properly
  • Mindless Repetition: Saying words without thinking about their meaning
  • Focusing Only on Numbers: Caring more about hitting a count than quality
  • Showing Off: Doing dhikr loudly to impress others
  • Irregular Practice: Doing lots of dhikr randomly instead of building a consistent habit
  • Using Unauthentic Formulas: Making up your own dhikr instead of using the Sunnah

A Simple Daily Dhikr Routine for Beginners

Morning (5 minutes):

  1. SubhanAllah (33 times)
  2. Alhamdulillah (33 times)
  3. Allahu Akbar (33 times)
  4. La ilaha illallah (10 times)

After Each Prayer (2 minutes):

  1. SubhanAllah (33 times)
  2. Alhamdulillah (33 times)
  3. Allahu Akbar (34 times)

Evening (5 minutes):

  1. Astaghfirullah (100 times)
  2. Recite Ayat al-Kursi
  3. Last three Surahs of the Quran (Al-Ikhlas, Al-Falaq, An-Nas)

Before Sleep (3 minutes):

  1. SubhanAllah (33 times)
  2. Alhamdulillah (33 times)
  3. Allahu Akbar (34 times)
  4. Recite Ayat al-Kursi

Advanced Practice: Group Dhikr

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "No people sit together remembering Allah, but the angels surround them, mercy covers them, tranquility descends upon them, and Allah mentions them to those who are with Him." (Muslim)

Gathering with others for dhikr is highly rewarded, as long as it's done according to the Sunnah and not in innovative ways.

Remember: The goal of dhikr is not just to move your tongue, but to bring life to your heart. Start small, be consistent, and let Allah's remembrance transform your life.

Take Action Today

Don't just read this guide and move on. Right now, make a commitment to incorporate just ONE dhikr routine from this article into your daily life. Start with the morning routine - it's only 5 minutes. Do it every day for one week, and you'll see the difference in your heart.
