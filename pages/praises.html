Praises of Allah

  1. Home
  2. Dhikr & Du'a
  3. Praises of Allah

Praising Allah (Hamd) and glorifying Him (Tasbih) are among the most beloved acts of worship that purify the heart and strengthen our connection with the Creator. These beautiful forms of remembrance bring immense spiritual rewards and peace.

The Prophet ﷺ taught us various ways to praise Allah throughout the day, each carrying unique blessings and benefits. These praises help us recognize Allah's greatness, express gratitude for His countless bounties, and maintain consciousness of His presence.

Regular recitation of these praises transforms the heart, increases faith (Iman), and serves as a means of earning Allah's pleasure and forgiveness. They are simple yet profound expressions of our love and reverence for Allah.

SubhanAllah - Glorifying Allah

سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ

(Anytime, especially 33x after prayers)

Glory be to Allah

Alternative: Allah is free from all imperfections

SubḥānAllāh

Beloved to the Most Merciful

The Prophet ﷺ said: "Two words are light on the tongue, weigh heavily in the scales, and are beloved to the Most Merciful: 'SubḥānAllāhi wa bi-ḥamdih, SubḥānAllāhi-l-ʿAẓīm' (Glory be to Allah and praise Him, Glory be to Allah the Magnificent)." (Bukhārī 6682, Muslim 2694)

Planting Trees in Paradise

The Prophet ﷺ said: "Whoever says 'SubḥānAllāh' (Glory be to Allah), a tree is planted for him in Paradise." (Tirmidhī 3464)

Most Frequent Dhikr of Angels

Angels continuously glorify Allah with "SubḥānAllāh," making this dhikr a way to join their constant worship. The Quran frequently mentions angels glorifying Allah day and night without fatigue.

Declaring Allah's Perfection

• "SubḥānAllāh" declares Allah free from all imperfections and defects

• Acknowledges Allah's absolute purity and transcendence

• Rejects any attribution of weakness, need, or limitation to Allah

Spiritual Purification

• Purifies the heart from doubts about Allah's perfection

• Cleanses the mind of inappropriate thoughts about the Creator

• Establishes proper reverence and awe for Allah

Recognition of Allah's Majesty

• Acknowledges Allah's absolute sovereignty and authority

• Recognizes Allah's infinite wisdom in all His actions

• Shows submission to Allah's perfect will and decree

Daily Practice Benefits

• Light on the tongue yet heavy in the scales of good deeds

• Can be recited silently anytime, anywhere

• Especially recommended 33 times after each prayer

• Increases mindfulness of Allah throughout the day

Alhamdulillah - All Praise to Allah

الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ

(Anytime, especially 33x after prayers)

All praise is for Allah

Alternative: All thanks and gratitude belong to Allah

Al-ḥamdu lillāh

The Best of Speech

The Prophet ﷺ said: "The best of remembrance is 'Lā ilāha illa-llāh' (There is no deity except Allah), and the best of supplication is 'Al-ḥamdu lillāh' (All praise is for Allah)." (Tirmidhī 3383, Ibn Mājah 3800)

Filling the Scales

The Prophet ﷺ said: "Al-ḥamdu lillāh fills the scales, and SubḥānAllāh and Al-ḥamdu lillāh fill what is between the heavens and the earth." (Muslim 223)

Opening of the Quran

Allah begins the greatest chapter of the Quran (al-Fātiḥah) with "Al-ḥamdu lillāhi Rabbi-l-ʿālamīn," showing the supreme importance of praising Allah. This makes "Alhamdulillah" a connection to the daily recitation in prayer.

Comprehensive Praise

• "Al-ḥamdu" encompasses all forms of praise and gratitude

• Includes praise for Allah's attributes, actions, and blessings

• Acknowledges Allah as the source of all good

Exclusive Attribution

• "Lillāh" - exclusively for Allah, not shared with anyone

• Recognizes Allah as the ultimate cause of all blessings

• Rejects crediting creation for Allah's bounties

Gratitude and Recognition

• Expresses gratitude for both apparent and hidden blessings

• Acknowledges Allah's continuous care and provision

• Shows appreciation even during difficulties (thanking Allah for His wisdom)

Transformative Power

• Changes perspective from complaint to gratitude

• Increases awareness of Allah's countless blessings

• Brings contentment and peace to the heart

• Opens doors for more blessings from Allah

Allahu Akbar - Allah is the Greatest

اللَّهُ أَكْبَرُ

(Anytime, especially 34x after prayers)

Allah is the Greatest

Alternative: Allah is greater than everything

Allāhu akbar

The Key to Every Prayer

"Allāhu akbar" is the opening of every prayer (Takbīr al-Iḥrām) and is repeated throughout each prayer, making it one of the most frequently uttered phrases by Muslims worldwide. It establishes the sacred nature of worship.

Greater Than All Concerns

The Prophet ﷺ would say "Allāhu akbar" when ascending hills during travel, symbolizing that Allah is greater than all obstacles and difficulties we face in life.

Call of Victory

Historically, "Allāhu akbar" has been the battle cry of Muslims, not as an act of aggression, but as a declaration that Allah's justice and truth are greater than all oppression and falsehood.

Most Comprehensive Dhikr

Unlike other phrases that specify what Allah is (like "SubḥānAllāh"), "Allāhu akbar" declares that Allah is greater than anything we can imagine or conceive, making it the most comprehensive form of praise.

Ultimate Supremacy

• "Akbar" (Greatest) - comparative form indicating superiority over all

• Allah is greater than any difficulty, fear, or challenge

• Allah's greatness surpasses human imagination and comprehension

Putting Life in Perspective

• Diminishes the importance of worldly concerns

• Provides comfort during trials and hardships

• Reminds us of Allah's absolute control over all affairs

Spiritual Elevation

• Elevates the soul above material attachments

• Creates awareness of Allah's magnificence

• Instills proper humility before the Creator

Practical Applications

• Begin difficult tasks with "Allāhu akbar" for strength

• Recite when facing fears or challenges

• Use during moments of joy to acknowledge Allah's blessings

• Remember that Allah is greater than any sin when seeking forgiveness

The Complete Formula of Praise

سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ وَبِحَمْدِهِ، سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ الْعَظِيمِ

(Morning and evening, anytime)

Glory be to Allah and praise Him, Glory be to Allah the Magnificent.

SubḥānAllāhi wa bi-ḥamdih, SubḥānAllāhi-l-ʿAẓīm.

Light on Tongue, Heavy in Scales

The Prophet ﷺ said: "Two words are light on the tongue, weigh heavily in the scales, and are beloved to the Most Merciful: 'SubḥānAllāhi wa bi-ḥamdih, SubḥānAllāhi-l-ʿAẓīm.'" (Bukhārī 6682, Muslim 2694)

Erasing Sins Like Ocean Foam

The Prophet ﷺ said: "Whoever says 'SubḥānAllāhi wa bi-ḥamdih' one hundred times during the day, his sins will be wiped away, even if they were like the foam of the sea." (Bukhārī 6405, Muslim 2691)

Beloved to Allah

This specific combination is mentioned as being particularly beloved to Allah, making it one of the most recommended forms of dhikr. It combines glorification (Tasbīḥ) with praise (Ḥamd) in perfect harmony.

Perfect Combination

• Combines tasbīḥ (glorification) and ḥamd (praise) together

• "Wa bi-ḥamdih" - and with His praise, indicating praise accompanies glorification

• Shows that Allah's perfection itself is praiseworthy

Acknowledging Allah's Magnificence

• "Al-ʿAẓīm" - The Magnificent, one of Allah's beautiful Names

• Emphasizes Allah's grandeur and supreme greatness

• Connects our praise to Allah's specific attribute of magnificence

Comprehensive Worship

• Encompasses both negation (freedom from defects) and affirmation (praise)

• Balances reverential fear with loving admiration

• Creates complete spiritual connection with Allah

Ease and Reward

• Simple to memorize and recite regularly

• Can be said silently throughout the day

• Brings immediate spiritual purification and peace

• Guarantees massive reward with minimal effort
