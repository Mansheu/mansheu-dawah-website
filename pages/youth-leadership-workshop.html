About This Event

The Youth Islamic Leadership Workshop is our flagship bootcamp dedicated to equipping 16-25 year-olds with prophetic leadership frameworks, practical organising skills, and a network of mentors. Across two immersive days, participants dissect the seerah's leadership lessons, practice persuasive communication, and design service projects tailored to their masjid or campus.

Facilitators from the Mansheu Youth Program blend case studies, simulation labs, and outdoor team challenges so attendees leave with confidence as well as a tangible action plan. Each cohort is capped at 60 to preserve coaching time, and alumni mentors shadow every table to provide feedback.

Graduates earn a certificate of completion, access to quarterly mastermind calls, and priority placement for the Mansheu Summer Fellowship. Meals, prayer breaks, and networking lounges are woven into the schedule to foster genuine brotherhood and sisterhood.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is eligible to attend?

The workshop is designed for Muslims aged 16-25 who want to serve their masjid, campus, or neighbourhood through leadership.

Are meals and prayers accommodated?

Yes. Breakfast, lunch, snacks, and hot drinks are provided on both days. Dedicated musalla spaces and wudu facilities are on-site.

Is there a dress code?

Smart casual attire suitable for prayer and outdoor activities. Please bring trainers for the leadership challenge.

How do scholarships work?

Submit a short statement explaining financial need. Decisions are shared within five working days and include optional travel stipends.

Will certificates be issued?

Yes, participants who attend both days and complete the project pitch receive a signed certificate and CPD letter.
