New Quran Study Circle Launches

By Editorial Team

Published: December 1, 2025 · Jumada al-Awwal 27, 1447

Updated: December 2, 2025 · Jumada al-Awwal 28, 1447

Responding to dozens of enrolment requests logged after the Eid retreat, Mansheu Dawah officially launched its new Quran study circle on Monday evening at the Knowledge Campus annex in Ogba.

The programme limits each cohort to 36 adults so instructors can offer personalised tajwid corrections and memorisation therapy. Seats for the pioneer class filled in 48 hours, prompting coordinators to open a waiting list for the March intake.

Curriculum Rooted in Tajwid and Application

Lead instructor Ustadh Karim Danjuma designed a six-month syllabus that marries makharij drills with thematic tafsir. Learners spend the first half-hour of every session reviewing articulation points before transitioning into reflective reading of Juz Amma, Yasin, and Rahman.

Scholars from the Lagos State Arabic Board endorsed the curriculum after reviewing its scaffolded assessments. Participants are graded on recitation accuracy, understanding of key vocabulary, and commitment to weekly service reflections.

Weekly Schedule and Enrollment

Classes hold on Mondays and Thursdays from 6:00 p.m. to 8:15 p.m., with optional Saturday revision circles led by certified alumni. Tuition is subsidised through sadaqah grants, keeping fees at NGN 12,000 per quarter while textbooks are supplied by the Mansheu Library Trust.

  • Makharij and sifaat clinic every first Monday
  • Living Quran tafsir lab focusing on social ethics
  • Hifdh journaling workshop that tracks triggers and progress
  • Peer-to-peer teaching slot where students coach each other
  • Monthly service practicum visiting elderly Muslims with recitations

Applicants submit a one-minute voice note and a short reflection on why they wish to join. Those with zero Arabic background receive a preparatory boot camp in January to master the alphabet and vowels before classes resume in February.

Hybrid Tools Support Memorisation

Each participant receives access to a dashboard that pairs the Mansheu digital counter with instructor voice notes. The portal hosts slow-speed recordings, tajwid quizzes, and attendance logs so mentors can intervene early when someone misses targets.

The programme also utilises a "buddy light" feature: a slim LED device placed atop mushafs that glows green when posture and recitation tempo align with preset metrics captured by the class microphone. Engineers from the Mansheu Labs Fellowship produced the prototype after testing it in youth halaqat.

Community Reaction and Next Steps

Parents from Ikorodu and Lekki drove to Ogba for the inaugural session, promising to lobby for satellite circles closer to their homes. The Education Board says it will survey interest in other districts once the first three months of data are analysed.

"We are not measuring success by how many ajza' are memorised, but by how gently students carry Allah's words into their speech and service. This circle is a workshop for hearts before it is a classroom for tongues."

Ustadh Karim Danjuma, Lead Instructor

Virtual seats will open next quarter for diaspora members who wish to join via livestream. Interested learners can join the waiting list on the Mansheu portal, with placement interviews scheduled after the Ramadan break.
