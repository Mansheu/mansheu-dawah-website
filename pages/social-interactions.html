Build bonds with prophetic etiquette: greetings of peace, responding to sneezing, and kind words.

Greeting of Peace

السَّلامُ عليكم ورحمةُ اللهِ وبركاتُه

(When meeting)

As‑salāmu ʿalaykum wa raḥmatullāhi wa barakātuh.

وَعليكم السَّلام ورحمةُ اللهِ وبركاتُه — And upon you be peace, Allah’s mercy and His blessings.

Source: Qur'an 4:86; Sunnah

Spreading salām fosters love and unity; it is a means to enter Paradise.

Initiate with the fuller greeting when possible; respond with equal or better.

When Sneezing

إذا عطسَ فقال: الحمدُ لله. قيل له: يرحمُكَ الله، فيقولُ: يهديكمُ اللهُ ويُصلِحُ بالَكم

(Etiquette)

The sneezer: Alḥamdu lillāh. Reply: Yarḥamuk Allāh. Response: Yahdīkumullāh wa yuṣliḥ bālakum.

The one who sneezes says: “Alhamdulillah.” The listener replies: “Yarḥamuk Allah (may Allah have mercy on you).” The sneezer then says: “Yahdīkumullāh wa yuṣliḥ bālakum (May Allah guide you and set your affairs right).”

Source: Sahih al‑Bukhari

Exchanging dua strengthens bonds and spreads mercy among believers.
