The Universe Glorifies Allah

8 min read

Everything is in Constant Remembrance

When we think of dhikr, we often limit it to human beings reciting the Names of Allah or saying SubhanAllah. But the Quran reveals an amazing reality: the entire universe is engaged in the remembrance of Allah! Every atom, every star, every living creature is glorifying Allah in ways we cannot fully comprehend.

The Quranic Evidence

تُسَبِّحُ لَهُ السَّمَاوَاتُ السَّبْعُ وَالْأَرْضُ وَمَن فِيهِنَّ ۚ وَإِن مِّن شَيْءٍ إِلَّا يُسَبِّحُ بِحَمْدِهِ وَلَٰكِن لَّا تَفْقَهُونَ تَسْبِيحَهُمْ

"The seven heavens and the earth and whatever is in them exalt Him. And there is not a thing except that it exalts [Allah] by His praise, but you do not understand their [way of] exalting." - Qur'an 17:44

This verse is profound. Allah explicitly tells us that EVERYTHING - not just living beings, but even non-living things - glorifies Him. The mountains, the rivers, the trees, the stones, the stars - all are in constant worship.

Examples from the Quran and Sunnah

The Mountains and Birds with Prophet Dawud (AS)

Allah says: "Indeed, We subjected the mountains [to praise] with him, exalting [Allah] in the evening and [after] sunrise. And the birds assembled, all with him repeating [praises]." (Quran 38:18-19)

When Prophet Dawud (David, peace be upon him) would glorify Allah, the mountains would join him! The birds would gather and participate in this celestial choir. Imagine - entire mountains echoing with the praise of Allah!

The Pebbles in the Prophet's Hand

The Companions narrated that pebbles would glorify Allah in the hand of Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ). They could actually hear the tasbih (glorification) of these small stones! This shows that even the smallest, seemingly insignificant things are engaged in dhikr.

The Thunder and Angels

"And the thunder exalts [Allah] with praise of Him - and the angels [as well] from fear of Him." (Quran 13:13)

The sound of thunder itself is a form of glorification! While we might hear it as just a loud noise, it is actually praising Allah. The angels join in this praise out of reverence and awe for their Creator.

How Does Creation Glorify Allah?

Scholars have explained that creation glorifies Allah in two ways:

1. Through Their State of Being (Tasbih of State)

Everything in existence points to Allah's greatness simply by existing. The perfect orbit of planets, the precise structure of atoms, the complexity of DNA, the beauty of a flower - all of these testify to Allah's wisdom, power, and perfection. This itself is a form of glorification.

2. Through Literal Praise (Tasbih of Speech)

The Quran tells us that creation has a form of speech that we don't understand. Trees, stones, animals - they all have a language of praise. Prophet Sulayman (Solomon) understood the language of birds and ants. The Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ) could hear the glorification of pebbles. Just because we can't hear or understand it doesn't mean it's not happening.

What This Means for Us

We Are Part of a Universal Choir

When you say "SubhanAllah," you're not doing it alone. You're joining a cosmic symphony that has been ongoing since the beginning of creation. The stars above you, the earth beneath you, the air around you - all are glorifying Allah with you. What an honor!

We Have Free Will

Unlike the rest of creation which glorifies Allah by nature and necessity, we have been given the choice. Angels can only obey. Animals and plants have no option. But humans can choose whether to remember Allah or forget Him. This makes our dhikr even more valuable - it's a conscious, deliberate act of worship.

We Are the Guardians of Earth

Allah placed us on Earth as His vicegerents (Khalifah). Part of our role is to recognize and appreciate the dhikr of all creation. When we see a tree, we should remember that it's glorifying Allah. When we hear thunder, we should join in its praise. When we look at the stars, we should reflect on their obedience to Allah.

Practical Applications

  • In Nature: When you see natural phenomena - sunrise, rain, stars, mountains - remember they are glorifying Allah. Join them by saying "SubhanAllah" or "Alhamdulillah."
  • With Animals: The Prophet (ﷺ) heard a camel complaining to him. He understood that animals have feelings and are conscious of Allah. Treat them with mercy and remember their tasbih.
  • Environmental Responsibility: Knowing that everything glorifies Allah should make us more careful about how we treat the environment. These are fellow worshippers of Allah!
  • Finding Allah Everywhere: You're never alone. Wherever you are, you're surrounded by creation that is constantly remembering Allah. This should increase your consciousness of Him.

The Shame of Heedlessness

Ibn Al-Qayyim, the great Islamic scholar, said: "How shameful it is that the person stays heedless of Allah while everything around him is remembering Him - the trees, the stones, the earth, the sky, the mountains, the birds. They are all engaged in dhikr while the human being, who was created specifically for worship, remains forgetful!"

A Humbling Perspective

Think about this: right now, as you read these words, galaxies millions of light-years away are glorifying Allah. Creatures in the deepest oceans are praising Him. Trees in remote forests are doing tasbih. And the question is: will you join them?

"Do you not see that Allah is exalted by whoever is within the heavens and the earth and [by] the birds with wings spread [in flight]? Each [of them] has known his [means of] prayer and exalting [Him], and Allah is Knowing of what they do." - Qur'an 24:41

The Choice is Ours

Everything in the universe has submitted to Allah and glorifies Him. The sun, moon, stars, trees, animals - all are in perfect harmony with Allah's will. Only humans and jinn have been given the choice. We can choose to join this universal chorus of praise, or we can choose to turn away.

Make the wise choice. Open your eyes to the dhikr happening all around you, and add your voice to this magnificent, eternal symphony of praise. Remember: every atom in existence is glorifying Allah. Will you?
