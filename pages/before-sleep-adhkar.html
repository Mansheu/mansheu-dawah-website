Before Sleep Adhkar

  1. Home
  2. Dhikr & Du'a
  3. Before Sleep Adhkar

Before surrendering to sleep, we turn to Allah with humble supplications seeking His protection and guidance. The bedtime adhkar are sacred remembrances that purify the soul, invoke divine protection, and ensure peaceful rest under Allah's care.

These blessed supplications were taught by the Prophet ﷺ to create a spiritual shield around us as we enter the vulnerable state of sleep. They help us end each day with gratitude, seek forgiveness, and place our complete trust in Allah.

Reciting these adhkar before sleep establishes a beautiful connection with Allah, bringing tranquility to the heart and protection from all harm throughout the night.

The Comprehensive Bedtime Du'a

اللَّهُمَّ بِاسْمِكَ أَمُوتُ وَأَحْيَا

(1x)

O Allah, with Your Name I die and I live.

Allāhumma bismika amūtu wa aḥyā.

The Prophet's Bedtime Practice

Hudhaifa (raḍiy Allāhu ʿanhu) reported: "When the Prophet ﷺ intended to go to sleep, he would place his right hand under his cheek and then say: 'Allāhumma bismika amūtu wa aḥyā' (O Allah, with Your Name I die and I live)."

And when he woke up, he would say: "Al-ḥamdu lillāhi-lladhī aḥyānā baʿda mā amātanā wa ilayhi-n-nushūr" (All praise is for Allah, Who gave us life after having taken it from us, and unto Him is the resurrection)." (Bukhārī 6312, 6314)

Placing Trust in Allah

This simple yet profound du'ā represents the believer's complete submission to Allah. By invoking Allah's Name before sleep, we acknowledge that our very existence - our sleeping and waking, our dying and living - are all in His Hands.

Sleep is often referred to as the "lesser death" in Islamic tradition, as the soul temporarily departs from conscious control. Through this du'ā, we place our trust in Allah during this vulnerable state.

The Meaning of "Dying" and "Living"

• Sleep is considered a form of temporary death in Islamic understanding

• "I die" refers to entering the state of sleep where consciousness departs

• "I live" refers to waking up with renewed life and consciousness

• Both states are entirely in Allah's control and by His permission

Invoking Allah's Name

• Beginning with "Bismika" (with Your Name) seeks Allah's protection and blessing

• Allah's Name encompasses all His beautiful attributes and qualities

• It creates a spiritual barrier against harm during sleep

• Shows complete reliance on Allah rather than our own strength

Spiritual Benefits

• Acknowledges Allah's complete sovereignty over life and death

• Develops tawakkul (reliance and trust) in Allah

• Purifies the heart from attachment to worldly concerns

• Prepares the soul for peaceful rest under divine protection

Physical Practice

• Place your right hand under your right cheek (following the Sunnah)

• Lie on your right side when possible

• Recite with presence of heart and sincere intention

• Make it the last thing you say before sleeping

Seeking Refuge Before Sleep

اللَّهُمَّ أَسْلَمْتُ وَجْهِي إِلَيْكَ، وَفَوَّضْتُ أَمْرِي إِلَيْكَ، وَأَلْجَأْتُ ظَهْرِي إِلَيْكَ، رَغْبَةً وَرَهْبَةً إِلَيْكَ، لاَ مَلْجَأَ وَلاَ مَنْجَا مِنْكَ إِلاَّ إِلَيْكَ، آمَنْتُ بِكِتَابِكَ الَّذِي أَنْزَلْتَ، وَبِنَبِيِّكَ الَّذِي أَرْسَلْتَ

(1x)

O Allah, I have surrendered my face to You, delegated my affairs to You, and relied upon You for support - out of desire for You and fear of You. There is no refuge or escape from You except to You. I believe in Your Book which You have revealed, and Your Prophet whom You have sent.

Allāhumma aslamtu wajhī ilayka, wa fawwadtu amrī ilayka, wa aljatu ẓahrī ilayka, raghbatan wa rahbatan ilayka, lā malja'a wa lā manjā minka illā ilayka, āmantu bi-kitābika-lladhī anzalta, wa bi-nabiyyika-lladhī arsalta.

The Last Words Before Sleep

The Prophet ﷺ said: "When one of you goes to bed, let him dust off his bed with the inside of his lower garment, for he does not know what came onto it after him. Then let him lie down on his right side and say: 'Allāhumma aslamtu wajhī ilayka...' (this du'ā)"

He ﷺ continued: "If he dies during that night, he will die in a state of the fiṭrah (natural state of faith). And make these words the last thing you say." (Bukhārī 247, Muslim 2710)

Dying Upon Faith

This du'ā serves as a renewal of faith and complete surrender to Allah. The Prophet ﷺ specifically emphasized making it "the last thing you say," highlighting its importance as a declaration of faith that could potentially be one's final words.

Those who recite this du'ā before sleep are guaranteed to die upon the fiṭrah (natural state of Islam) if Allah takes their soul during the night.

Complete Surrender to Allah

• "Aslamtu wajhī" - surrendering one's face represents complete submission

• The face represents one's honor, identity, and entire being

• "Fawwadtu amrī" - delegating all affairs shows complete trust

• "Aljatu ẓahrī" - relying on Allah for support and protection

Balance of Hope and Fear

• "Raghbatan wa rahbatan" - out of desire and fear

• Desire for Allah's mercy, forgiveness, and paradise

• Fear of Allah's displeasure and punishment

• This balance keeps the believer humble yet hopeful

Acknowledging Allah's Absolute Power

• "Lā malja'a wa lā manjā minka illā ilayka" - ultimate refuge is only with Allah

• No escape from Allah's decree except by turning to Him

• Recognition that all power and authority belong to Allah alone

Reaffirming Faith

• Belief in the Qur'ān as Allah's revealed book

• Belief in the Prophet ﷺ as Allah's final messenger

• This declaration of faith could be one's final testimony

• Ensures dying upon the correct belief if taken during sleep

The Simple Protection Formula

بِاسْمِكَ رَبِّي وَضَعْتُ جَنْبِي، وَبِكَ أَرْفَعُهُ، فَإِنْ أَمْسَكْتَ نَفْسِي فَارْحَمْهَا، وَإِنْ أَرْسَلْتَهَا فَاحْفَظْهَا بِمَا تَحْفَظُ بِهِ عِبَادَكَ الصَّالِحِينَ

(1x)

In Your Name, my Lord, I lay down my side, and by You I raise it up. If You take my soul, have mercy on it, and if You send it back, protect it with that which You protect Your righteous servants.

Bismika Rabbī waḍaʿtu janbī, wa bika arfaʿuh. Fa-in amsakta nafsī fa-rḥamhā, wa in arsaltahā fa-ḥfaẓhā bi-mā taḥfaẓu bihi ʿibādaka-ṣ-ṣāliḥīn.

A Beautiful Balance of Hope and Surrender

This du'ā was also taught by the Prophet ﷺ and is narrated in both Bukhārī and Muslim. It beautifully balances our acknowledgment that life and death are entirely in Allah's hands, while seeking His mercy and protection.

The du'ā covers both possibilities - whether Allah takes our soul during sleep (death) or returns it to us (waking up). In both cases, we seek Allah's care and protection.

Following the Righteous

By asking Allah to protect us "with that which You protect Your righteous servants," we align ourselves with the company of the pious and seek the same divine protection they receive.

This phrase also serves as an aspiration to be among Allah's righteous servants who are specially protected and cared for.

Physical and Spiritual Positioning

• "Waḍaʿtu janbī" - laying down one's side in Allah's Name

• Physical act of sleeping becomes an act of worship and trust

• "Wa bika arfaʿuh" - waking up is also by Allah's permission and help

• Every movement, rest, and consciousness is under Allah's control

Preparation for Both Outcomes

• "In amsakta nafsī" - if Allah takes the soul (death during sleep)

• "Fa-rḥamhā" - seeking Allah's mercy in the afterlife

• "In arsaltahā" - if Allah sends the soul back (waking up)

• "Fa-ḥfaẓhā" - seeking protection for the coming day

Divine Protection Pattern

• Asking for the same protection given to righteous servants

• This includes protection from sin, harm, and spiritual corruption

• Seeking to be in the company of the righteous

• Aspiring to achieve the same level of divine care

Comprehensive Coverage

• Covers the moment of lying down and waking up

• Addresses both death and continued life scenarios

• Seeks mercy for the soul and protection for the body

• Comprehensive du'ā for complete divine care
