Salawat

  1. Home
  2. Dhikr & Du'a
  3. Salawat

Salawat (صلوات) - sending blessings upon Prophet Muhammad ﷺ - is one of the most beloved acts of worship in Islam. Allah Himself sends His blessings upon the Prophet, and He commands the believers to do the same.

The Quran states: "Indeed, Allah and His angels send blessings upon the Prophet. O you who believe! Send blessings upon him and greet him with abundant peace." (Al-Ahzab 33:56)

Regularly reciting Salawat brings immense spiritual rewards, purifies the heart, increases love for the Prophet ﷺ, and serves as a means of intercession on the Day of Judgment. It is a beautiful way to express our gratitude for the guidance brought by our beloved Messenger ﷺ.

Simple Salawat

اللَّهُمَّ صَلِّ عَلَى مُحَمَّدٍ

(Anytime, especially during daily prayers)

O Allah, send blessings upon Muhammad

Allāhumma ṣalli ʿalā Muḥammad

Ten Blessings in Return

The Prophet ﷺ said: "Whoever sends blessings upon me once, Allah will send blessings upon him ten times, remove ten sins from him, and raise him ten degrees." (Tirmidhī 484, Nasā'ī 1297)

Forgiveness of Sins

The Prophet ﷺ said: "Whoever says 'Allāhumma ṣalli ʿalā Muḥammadin wa ʿalā āli Muḥammad' (O Allah, send blessings upon Muhammad and upon the family of Muhammad), Allah will forgive his sins." (Ibn Mājah 906)

Closeness on Day of Judgment

The Prophet ﷺ said: "The people most worthy of my intercession on the Day of Resurrection will be those who said 'Lā ilāha illa-llāh' sincerely from their hearts, and those who sent the most blessings upon me." (Bukhārī 99)

Meaning of Salawat

• "Ṣalli" - Allah's blessing includes honor, praise, and elevation of status

• When we ask Allah to send blessings on the Prophet ﷺ, we're asking for his continued honor

• This acknowledges the Prophet's ﷺ special status with Allah

Spiritual Connection

• Creates a spiritual bond between the believer and the Prophet ﷺ

• Increases love and reverence for the final Messenger

• Purifies the heart from arrogance and neglect

Daily Practice

• Recite whenever the Prophet's ﷺ name is mentioned

• Include in daily dhikr and prayers

• Especially recommended on Fridays

• Simple yet profoundly rewarding

Complete Salawat - Salat Ibrahimiyyah

اللَّهُمَّ صَلِّ عَلَى مُحَمَّدٍ وَعَلَى آلِ مُحَمَّدٍ كَمَا صَلَّيْتَ عَلَى إِبْرَاهِيمَ وَعَلَى آلِ إِبْرَاهِيمَ إِنَّكَ حَمِيدٌ مَجِيدٌ، اللَّهُمَّ بَارِكْ عَلَى مُحَمَّدٍ وَعَلَى آلِ مُحَمَّدٍ كَمَا بَارَكْتَ عَلَى إِبْرَاهِيمَ وَعَلَى آلِ إِبْرَاهِيمَ إِنَّكَ حَمِيدٌ مَجِيدٌ

(Taught by the Prophet ﷺ, recited in daily prayers)

O Allah, send blessings upon Muhammad and upon the family of Muhammad as You sent blessings upon Ibrahim and upon the family of Ibrahim. Indeed, You are Praiseworthy and Glorious. O Allah, bless Muhammad and the family of Muhammad as You blessed Ibrahim and the family of Ibrahim. Indeed, You are Praiseworthy and Glorious.

Allāhumma ṣalli ʿalā Muḥammadin wa ʿalā āli Muḥammad, kamā ṣallayta ʿalā Ibrāhīma wa ʿalā āli Ibrāhīm, innaka Ḥamīdun Majīd. Allāhumma bārik ʿalā Muḥammadin wa ʿalā āli Muḥammad, kamā bārakta ʿalā Ibrāhīma wa ʿalā āli Ibrāhīm, innaka Ḥamīdun Majīd.

Taught by the Prophet ﷺ Himself

When the companions asked: "O Messenger of Allah, we have learned how to greet you with peace, but how should we send blessings upon you?" The Prophet ﷺ taught them this complete formula. (Bukhārī 3370, Muslim 406)

Complete and Perfect Form

This is the most complete and perfect form of Salawat, combining both sending blessings (Ṣalāh) and seeking Allah's special favor (Barakah) upon the Prophet ﷺ and his family.

Connection to Ibrahim عليه السلام

By referencing Ibrahim عليه السلام, we acknowledge the continuity of Allah's favor upon His chosen messengers and connect our Prophet ﷺ to the father of monotheistic faith.

Two Parts of Complete Salawat

• Ṣalāh - Asking Allah to honor and elevate the Prophet ﷺ

• Barakah - Asking Allah to bless and increase his honor

• Both requests include the Prophet's family (Āl Muḥammad)

Reference to Ibrahim عليه السلام

• Shows the continuity of Allah's mercy upon His messengers

• Ibrahim عليه السلام was the friend of Allah (Khalīlullāh)

• Creates a connection between all the great prophets

Allah's Beautiful Names

• Ḥamīd - The Praiseworthy, deserving all praise

• Majīd - The Glorious, possessing absolute honor and nobility

• These names affirm Allah's worthiness to grant such blessings

Prophetic Instruction

• This exact formula was taught by the Prophet ﷺ when asked

• Used in the Tashahhud (final sitting) of every prayer

• Represents the most authentic and complete way to send Salawat

Special Friday Salawat

اللَّهُمَّ صَلِّ وَسَلِّمْ وَبَارِكْ عَلَى سَيِّدِنَا مُحَمَّدٍ وَعَلَى آلِهِ وَصَحْبِهِ أَجْمَعِينَ

(Especially on Fridays and blessed times)

O Allah, send blessings, peace, and barakah upon our master Muhammad and upon his family and all his companions.

Allāhumma ṣalli wa sallim wa bārik ʿalā sayyidinā Muḥammadin wa ʿalā ālihi wa ṣaḥbihi ajmaʿīn

Special Merit on Friday

The Prophet ﷺ said: "Send blessings upon me abundantly on Friday, for your blessings will be presented to me." (Abū Dāwūd 1047, Ibn Mājah 1085)

Comprehensive Blessing

This formula combines three elements: Ṣalāh (blessings), Salām (peace), and Barakah (divine favor), making it a comprehensive form of honoring the Prophet ﷺ.

Including the Companions

By including "his family and all his companions," we honor those who preserved and transmitted Islam to us, showing our gratitude for their sacrifices and dedication.

Triple Request

• Ṣalli - Send blessings and honor

• Sallim - Grant peace and safety

• Bārik - Bestow continuous divine favor

Respectful Address

• "Sayyidinā" - Our master, showing utmost respect

• Acknowledges the Prophet's ﷺ leadership and guidance

• Expresses our love and reverence properly

Comprehensive Inclusion

• "Ālihi" - His pure family members

• "Ṣaḥbihi" - His noble companions

• "Ajmaʿīn" - All of them without exception

Friday's Special Status

• Friday is the most blessed day of the week

• Salawat recited on Friday reaches the Prophet ﷺ

• Recommended to increase Salawat throughout Friday
