Occasions When Du'a is Readily Accepted

9 min read

Golden Moments for Your Supplications

While Allah hears every supplication at all times, there are certain blessed moments when He has promised that du'as are especially likely to be accepted. The Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ) taught us about these precious times so we could maximize the effectiveness of our prayers. Knowing these occasions is like having a treasure map to divine acceptance.

1. The Last Third of the Night

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "Our Lord descends to the lowest heaven during the last third of every night, and He says: 'Who is calling upon Me that I may answer him? Who is asking from Me that I may give him? Who is seeking My forgiveness that I may forgive him?'" (Bukhari & Muslim)

How to calculate it: Divide the time between Isha and Fajr into three parts. The last third is the golden period. For example, if Isha is at 9 PM and Fajr is at 6 AM (9 hours), the last third begins at 3 AM.

2. Between Adhan and Iqamah

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "Du'a made between the adhan and iqamah is not rejected." (Abu Dawud, Tirmidhi)

This brief window before each of the five daily prayers is a guaranteed opportunity. Don't waste these precious minutes on your phone - use them to ask Allah for your needs!

3. While Prostrating in Prayer

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "The closest that a servant is to his Lord is when he is in prostration, so increase du'a therein." (Muslim)

In sujood, you are in the most humble position possible, with your face on the ground before Allah. This is an ideal moment for heartfelt supplication. Make du'a in your own language during voluntary prayers or after the prescribed adhkar in obligatory prayers.

4. On Friday

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "On Friday there is an hour during which a Muslim does not ask Allah for anything but He will give it to him." (Bukhari & Muslim)

Most likely times:

  • From when the imam sits on the minbar until the prayer is over
  • The last hour before Maghrib

Make it a habit to set aside time every Friday afternoon for du'a!

5. When Rain Falls

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "Two du'as are not rejected: du'a at the time of the call to prayer, and du'a at the time of rain." (Abu Dawud)

Rain is a mercy from Allah, and when it falls, the doors of mercy are wide open. Don't just run for shelter - pause for a moment to raise your hands and make du'a!

6. When Breaking Fast

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "The fasting person has a du'a that is not rejected at the time of breaking fast." (Ibn Majah)

Whether during Ramadan or voluntary fasting, the moment when you're about to break your fast is extremely blessed. Have your du'a ready before you eat!

7. While Fasting

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "Three du'as are not rejected: the du'a of a fasting person, the du'a of a just ruler, and the du'a of one who has been wronged." (Ahmad, Tirmidhi)

Throughout the day while fasting, not just at iftar, your du'a has special acceptance. Use this entire blessed time!

8. Night of Qadr (Laylatul Qadr)

This night, during the last ten days of Ramadan, is better than a thousand months. The Prophet (ﷺ) taught Aisha (RA) to say: "Allahumma innaka 'afuwwun tuhibbul 'afwa fa'fu 'anni" (O Allah, You are Most Forgiving, and You love forgiveness, so forgive me).

9. During Travel

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "Three du'as are answered without doubt: the du'a of one who has been wronged, the du'a of the traveler, and the du'a of a parent for their child." (Abu Dawud, Tirmidhi)

Traveling is a time of difficulty and need, which makes the traveler's du'a especially powerful. Make abundant du'a during your journeys!

10. A Parent's Du'a for Their Child

As mentioned in the hadith above, a parent's du'a for their child is among those guaranteed to be answered. Parents, use this power wisely - pray FOR your children, not against them!

11. Du'a of One Who Has Been Wronged

The Prophet (ﷺ) said to Mu'adh: "Beware of the du'a of the oppressed, for there is no barrier between it and Allah." (Bukhari & Muslim)

This teaches us two lessons: If you've been wronged, your du'a is powerful. If you wrong others, fear their du'a against you!

12. After Reciting Certain Quranic Verses

After reciting Ayat al-Kursi, the last two verses of Surah Al-Baqarah, or other powerful verses, making du'a is especially effective.

13. On the Day of Arafah

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "The best of du'a is du'a on the day of Arafah." (Tirmidhi)

This applies not just to pilgrims, but to everyone. Fast on this day and spend it in abundant du'a!

14. After Obligatory Prayers

The time immediately after completing the five daily prayers is blessed for supplication. The Prophet (ﷺ) would make du'a after prayers, and we should follow his example.

15. When Waking Up at Night

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "Whoever wakes up at night and says 'La ilaha illallahu wahdahu la shareeka lah...' (There is no deity except Allah alone with no partner...) and then makes du'a, his du'a will be answered." (Bukhari)

16. During Difficulty and Distress

Allah says in the Quran: "And when My servants ask you concerning Me, indeed I am near. I respond to the invocation of the supplicant when he calls upon Me." (2:186)

Times of hardship are when we turn to Allah most sincerely, and He loves to answer during these moments.

17. When Drinking Zamzam Water

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "The water of Zamzam is for whatever purpose it is drunk for." (Ibn Majah)

Make a sincere intention and du'a when drinking Zamzam!

18. When Hearing the Rooster Crow

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "When you hear the crowing of a rooster, ask Allah for His bounty, for it has seen an angel." (Bukhari & Muslim)

19. In the Middle of the Night

Besides the last third, any night worship and du'a is beloved to Allah, especially when people are asleep and you wake up to pray in private.

20. When Saying "Ameen" After the Imam

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "When the imam says 'Ameen,' say 'Ameen,' for whoever says 'Ameen' at the same time as the angels, his previous sins will be forgiven." (Bukhari & Muslim)

Practical Tips to Seize These Moments

  1. Keep a Du'a List: Write down your most important needs so you're ready when these times arrive
  2. Set Reminders: Use your phone to remind you of these blessed times
  3. Learn Key Du'as: Memorize important supplications in Arabic
  4. Don't Rush: Take your time in these moments - don't just quickly recite and move on
  5. Include Others: Make du'a for your family, friends, and the Ummah
  6. Be Sincere: The most important factor is sincerity, not eloquence

"And your Lord says: 'Call upon Me; I will respond to you.'" - Qur'an 40:60

Remember

While these times are especially blessed, Allah hears every du'a at every moment. Never feel that a time is "wrong" for making du'a. However, being strategic and utilizing these golden opportunities shows wisdom and sincerity in seeking Allah's help.

Start by choosing 2-3 of these occasions that fit easily into your routine, and consistently make du'a during them. Gradually add more as they become habits. Remember: the key is consistency, not quantity!
