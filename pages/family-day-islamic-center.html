About This Event

Family Day transforms the Mansheu Dawah grounds into a mini-festival where toddlers, teens, and grandparents share one joyful space. Attractions stretch from inflatable obstacle courses to a serene elders' lounge, while the masjid courtyard hosts continuous Qur'an recitation and dhikr circles. Every activity is designed around shari'ah-compliant fun so families can unwind without compromise.

The Community Team has curated halal food trucks, artisan vendors, and a family photo booth to create new memories after Eid. Sports coaches will run friendly matches and tug-of-war heats, and the education wing will host story time and STEM stations for curious children. Throughout the day, stage hosts remind guests of prayer times and share short reflections on gratitude.

All proceeds from vendor fees support the Mansheu Relief Pantry. Attendance is free, but we encourage families to register so we can prepare sufficient welcome packs, face-painting supplies, and volunteer coverage.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the event really free?

Yes, entry and most activities are free. Vendors donate a small fee to support the Mansheu Relief Pantry.

What should we bring?

Bring picnic blankets, reusable water bottles, prayer mats, and sunscreen. Modest swimwear is required for the splash pad.

Are pets allowed?

Only registered assistance animals are permitted. Please email ahead if you plan to bring one so we can support you.

Will there be prayer facilities?

Yes, the masjid prayer halls remain open with additional wudu stations outdoors. Maghrib jama'ah concludes the day.

What if it rains?

Large marquees cover key zones, and indoor contingency rooms are prepared. Updates are sent via SMS to all registered families.
