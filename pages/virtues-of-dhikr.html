The Virtues of Dhikr

7 min read

The Immense Rewards of Remembering Allah

Dhikr (remembrance of Allah) is one of the most beloved acts of worship in Islam. Allah (سبحانه وتعالى) has mentioned its virtues extensively in the Quran, and the Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ) emphasized its importance throughout his life. The rewards for dhikr are immense and the benefits are both spiritual and worldly.

Virtues Mentioned in the Quran

Allah says in the Quran:

فَاذْكُرُونِي أَذْكُرْكُمْ وَاشْكُرُوا لِي وَلَا تَكْفُرُونِ

"So remember Me; I will remember you. And be grateful to Me and do not deny Me." - Qur'an 2:152

This verse shows the amazing reciprocity - when we remember Allah, He remembers us!

Ten Major Virtues of Dhikr

  1. Allah Remembers You: The greatest virtue is that Allah mentions you among the angels when you remember Him
  2. Peace of Heart: "Unquestionably, by the remembrance of Allah hearts are assured" (Quran 13:28)
  3. Protection from Shaytan: Dhikr is a shield against the whispers and traps of Satan
  4. Forgiveness of Sins: The Prophet (ﷺ) taught us specific adhkar that erase sins completely
  5. Easy to Practice: Unlike other acts of worship, dhikr can be done anytime, anywhere, in any state
  6. Multiplied Rewards: Some forms of dhikr earn rewards equivalent to freeing slaves or giving charity
  7. Light in the Grave: Dhikr will be a source of light and comfort in the darkness of the grave
  8. Intercession on Judgment Day: The Quran and dhikr will intercede for those who practiced them
  9. Removal of Anxiety: Regular dhikr removes worry, anxiety, and depression
  10. Nearness to Allah: Nothing brings you closer to Allah than His remembrance

Prophetic Traditions on Dhikr

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "Shall I not inform you of the best of your deeds, the purest of them with your Master, the loftiest of them in your ranks, better for you than spending gold and silver, and better for you than meeting your enemy and striking their necks and they strike your necks?" They said: "Of course." He said: "The remembrance of Allah, the Most High." (Tirmidhi)

The Lightest Yet Most Rewarding Act

One of the beautiful aspects of dhikr is that it requires no physical strength, no wealth, and no specific place. You can do dhikr while:

  • Walking, sitting, or lying down
  • Cooking, driving, or working
  • In a state of wudu or without
  • At home, at work, or traveling
  • In times of ease and in times of difficulty

Types of Dhikr and Their Specific Virtues

SubhanAllah (Glory be to Allah): The Prophet (ﷺ) said it fills the scales of good deeds

Alhamdulillah (All praise is for Allah): It fills the space between the heavens and earth

Allahu Akbar (Allah is the Greatest): Superior to everything upon which the sun rises

La ilaha illallah (There is no deity except Allah): The best of all dhikr and the key to Paradise

A Daily Practice

The Companions of the Prophet (ﷺ) would count that they sought Allah's forgiveness 100 times in a day. The Prophet himself would do dhikr constantly, even while going about his daily activities. Make dhikr a habit, and you will find that your heart becomes attached to Allah in a way you never imagined possible.

Remember: The one who remembers Allah among those who forget Him is like a living person among the dead.

Start Today

Begin with simple adhkar - say SubhanAllah, Alhamdulillah, and Allahu Akbar 33 times each after every prayer. Gradually build your practice. The key is consistency, not quantity. Even a little dhikr done regularly is better than a lot done occasionally.
