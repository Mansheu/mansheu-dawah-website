The Etiquette of Making Du'a

8 min read

Proper Manners When Calling Upon Allah

Making du'a is not just about asking - it's about how you ask. The way you approach Allah, the state of your heart, and the manner of your supplication all matter. The Prophet Muhammad (?) taught us the beautiful etiquettes of du'a that increase the likelihood of acceptance and demonstrate our respect and love for Allah.

1. Begin with Praise and Gratitude

Start your du'a by praising Allah and acknowledging His greatness. The Prophet (?) heard a man make du'a immediately without praising Allah or sending salutations upon the Prophet, and he said: "This man has rushed." (Abu Dawud, Tirmidhi)

How to do it:

  • Say "Alhamdulillah" (All praise is for Allah)
  • Mention Allah's beautiful names: "Ya Rahman, Ya Raheem" (O Most Merciful, O Most Compassionate)
  • Acknowledge His blessings upon you
  • Express gratitude for what you already have

2. Send Salutations Upon the Prophet (?)

The Prophet (?) said: "Every du'a is kept in abeyance until you send blessings upon the Prophet." (Tabarani)

Say: "Allahumma salli 'ala Muhammad wa 'ala aali Muhammad" (O Allah, send blessings upon Muhammad and the family of Muhammad)

Best practice: Send salutations at the beginning AND end of your du'a.

3. Face the Qiblah

When making du'a, it's recommended to face the direction of the Ka'bah in Makkah. This shows unity with all Muslims and respect for the sacred house of Allah.

4. Raise Your Hands

The Prophet (?) said: "Allah is Generous and Shy. When His servant raises his hands to Him, He is shy to return them empty." (Abu Dawud, Tirmidhi)

How to raise them:

  • Palms facing upward, as if receiving something
  • Hands raised to shoulder level or higher
  • After du'a, wipe your hands over your face

5. Be in a State of Purity (Recommended)

While wudu (ablution) is not required for du'a, it's highly recommended. Being clean and in wudu shows respect and helps you concentrate better.

6. Choose the Best Times

Make du'a during blessed times when acceptance is more likely:

  • Last third of the night
  • Between adhan and iqamah
  • While prostrating
  • On Friday
  • When it rains
  • While fasting

7. Lower Your Voice

Allah says: "Call upon your Lord in humility and privately." (Qur'an 7:55)

Don't make du'a too loudly. A moderate, humble tone is best - loud enough that you can hear yourself, but not shouting.

8. Be Humble and Submissive

Approach Allah with complete humility. You are a servant asking the All-Powerful Creator. Show your need, your weakness, and your dependence on Him.

The Prophet (?) said: "Allah does not accept du'a from an inattentive, heedless heart." (Tirmidhi)

9. Use Allah's Beautiful Names

Allah says: "And to Allah belong the best names, so invoke Him by them." (Qur'an 7:180)

When asking for mercy, say "Ya Raheem" (O Most Merciful). When seeking provision, say "Ya Razzaq" (O Provider). Match the name to your request!

10. Be Specific in Your Request

While general du'as are good, being specific is better. Don't just say "give me success" - specify what success you want: in your exams, in your business, in your marriage, etc.

11. Ask with Certainty and Hope

The Prophet (?) said: "Make du'a to Allah while being certain of a response." (Tirmidhi)

Don't say "O Allah, if You will, please..." - this shows doubt. Say "O Allah, please..." with full conviction that He will answer.

12. Repeat Your Du'a Three Times

The Prophet (?) would often repeat his du'a three times. This shows eagerness and persistence.

13. Make Du'a for Others First

The Prophet (?) said: "The du'a of a Muslim for his brother in his absence is readily accepted. An angel is appointed to his side; whenever he makes a good du'a for his brother, the appointed angel says 'Ameen, and may you also be blessed with the same.'" (Muslim)

Start by praying for your parents, family, friends, and the Muslim Ummah - then Allah will give you the same!

14. Acknowledge Your Sins

Before asking for what you want, acknowledge your shortcomings and ask for forgiveness. Say "Astaghfirullah" (I seek Allah's forgiveness) and admit your need for His mercy.

15. Don't Ask for Anything Sinful

The Prophet (?) said: "The du'a of any one of you will be answered so long as he is not impatient and says 'I made du'a but it was not answered.'" (Bukhari & Muslim)

Also, don't ask for anything that involves sin, breaking family ties, or harm to others.

16. Be Persistent and Patient

Don't give up if you don't see immediate results. Keep making the same du'a consistently. The Prophet (?) said persistence in du'a is beloved to Allah.

17. Use Quranic Du'as

The du'as mentioned in the Quran are guaranteed to be perfect. Learn and use them! For example:

  • "Rabbana atina fid-dunya hasanah wa fil-akhirati hasanah wa qina 'adhaban-nar" (Our Lord, give us good in this world and good in the Hereafter, and protect us from the punishment of the Fire) - 2:201

18. Cry If You Can

The Prophet (?) said: "Two eyes shall not be touched by the Fire: an eye that wept out of fear of Allah, and an eye that spent the night guarding in the way of Allah." (Tirmidhi)

If tears come naturally while making du'a, this is a sign of sincerity and softness of heart.

19. After Du'a, Say "Ameen"

This means "O Allah, accept my du'a." It seals your supplication.

20. Earn Halal Income

The Prophet (?) mentioned a traveler making du'a with raised hands, but his food, drink, and clothing were from haram sources, and he said: "How can his du'a be accepted?" (Muslim)

Ensure your earnings and consumption are halal for maximum acceptance of du'a.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Making du'a while neglecting obligatory acts (like salah)
  • Only making du'a during difficulties
  • Using rhyming words or being overly poetic (be natural and sincere)
  • Making du'a against yourself or your family in anger
  • Giving up too quickly
  • Only asking for worldly things (balance dunya and akhirah)

The Complete Structure of a Perfect Du'a

  1. Begin with "Bismillah"
  2. Praise Allah and mention His attributes
  3. Send salutations upon the Prophet (?)
  4. Acknowledge your sins and ask for forgiveness
  5. Present your needs and requests
  6. Ask for good in this life and the next
  7. Send salutations upon the Prophet (?) again
  8. End with "Ameen"
  9. Wipe your hands over your face

Remember

These etiquettes are not strict requirements - Allah accepts du'a from anyone, even if they don't follow all these rules. However, following these etiquettes shows respect, increases acceptance, and demonstrates that you truly value this precious gift of communication with Allah.

The most important thing is sincerity. A simple, heartfelt "Ya Allah, help me" from a sincere heart is better than a perfectly structured du'a made mechanically.

Start implementing these etiquettes one by one. You don't need to master them all at once. The beauty of Islam is that it's practical and gradual. Make du'a now, as you are, and gradually improve your approach over time.
