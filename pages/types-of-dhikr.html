The Types of Dhikr

8 min read

A Comprehensive Guide to Forms of Remembrance

Dhikr is not a single, uniform practice. Rather, it encompasses various forms and categories, each with its own characteristics, benefits, and occasions. Understanding these different types helps us enrich our spiritual practice and connect with Allah in diverse ways throughout our day.

1. Dhikr of the Tongue (Dhikr bil-Lisan)

This is the most common and well-known form - verbal remembrance of Allah. It includes:

The Core Phrases:

  • SubhanAllah (سبحان الله): "Glory be to Allah" - Glorifying Allah's perfection
  • Alhamdulillah (الحمد لله): "All praise is for Allah" - Expressing gratitude
  • Allahu Akbar (الله أكبر): "Allah is the Greatest" - Magnifying Allah
  • La ilaha illallah (لا إله إلا الله): "There is no deity except Allah" - Affirming Tawhid

Comprehensive Formulas:

  • Istighfar: "Astaghfirullah" (I seek Allah's forgiveness)
  • Hawqalah: "La hawla wa la quwwata illa billah" (No might nor power except with Allah)
  • Basmala: "Bismillah" (In the name of Allah)
  • Hasbala: "Hasbunallahu wa ni'mal wakeel" (Allah is sufficient for us, and He is the best Disposer of affairs)

2. Dhikr of the Heart (Dhikr bil-Qalb)

This is internal remembrance - keeping Allah in your thoughts and consciousness. It includes:

  • Contemplating Allah's names and attributes
  • Reflecting on His creation and blessings
  • Maintaining awareness of His presence (Ihsan)
  • Pondering the meanings of verses and adhkar
  • Feeling love, fear, and hope in relation to Allah

The Prophet (ﷺ) taught us that true remembrance combines both tongue and heart. Ibn Al-Qayyim said: "Dhikr of the tongue without the heart's presence is like a body without a soul."

3. Dhikr Through Actions (Dhikr bil-Jawārih)

Remembering Allah through physical acts of worship and good deeds:

  • Prayer (Salah): The most complete form of dhikr using body, tongue, and heart
  • Reciting Quran: The supreme form of verbal dhikr
  • Good deeds: Helping others, being just, showing kindness
  • Avoiding sins: Obedience to Allah's commands is a form of remembrance

4. Time-Specific Adhkar

Morning Adhkar (Adhkar as-Sabah)

Recited after Fajr prayer until sunrise. These include Ayat al-Kursi, last three Surahs of Quran, and various prophetic supplications for protection and blessings throughout the day.

Evening Adhkar (Adhkar al-Masa)

Recited after Asr prayer until sunset. Similar to morning adhkar but with focus on protection during the night.

Before Sleep Adhkar

Special dhikr for protection while sleeping, including Ayat al-Kursi, last two verses of Surah Al-Baqarah, and the tasbih of Fatimah (33 SubhanAllah, 33 Alhamdulillah, 34 Allahu Akbar).

Upon Waking

"Alhamdulillah alladhi ahyana ba'da ma amatana wa ilayhin-nushur" (Praise be to Allah who gave us life after death and to Him is the resurrection).

5. Occasion-Specific Adhkar

After Obligatory Prayers

The Prophet (ﷺ) taught specific adhkar after each of the five daily prayers, including 33 SubhanAllah, 33 Alhamdulillah, 34 Allahu Akbar, Ayat al-Kursi, and specific duas.

Travel Adhkar

Special supplications when starting a journey, including: "SubhanAlladhi sakhkhara lana hadha..." (Glory be to Him who has subjected this to us...).

Eating and Drinking

"Bismillah" before eating, and "Alhamdulillah" after finishing.

Entering and Leaving Places

Different adhkar for entering/leaving home, mosque, bathroom, etc.

6. Unrestricted Dhikr (Dhikr Mutlaq)

This is general remembrance not tied to specific times or occasions. You can do this anytime, anywhere:

  • Saying SubhanAllah while walking
  • Saying Alhamdulillah when you see something beautiful
  • Saying Astaghfirullah repeatedly
  • Sending salutations upon the Prophet (ﷺ)

7. Restricted Dhikr (Dhikr Muqayyad)

Dhikr that must be done at specific times or in specific ways, such as:

  • The adhkar said during prayer (in ruku, sujud, etc.)
  • The specific 100 times istighfar recommended daily
  • The prescribed adhkar after prayers
  • Dhikr during Hajj (Talbiyah, Takbir at Mina, etc.)

8. Silent vs. Audible Dhikr

Silent Dhikr (Dhikr Khafi)

  • More sincere and less prone to showing off
  • Can be done anywhere without disturbing others
  • Requires more concentration

Audible Dhikr (Dhikr Jahri)

  • Helps maintain focus
  • Can inspire others
  • Engages both tongue and ears
  • The Prophet (ﷺ) often did audible dhikr

9. Individual vs. Collective Dhikr

Individual Dhikr

Most personal adhkar are done individually. This allows for deep concentration and personal connection with Allah.

Collective Dhikr

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "No people sit together remembering Allah, but the angels surround them, mercy covers them, tranquility descends upon them, and Allah mentions them to those who are with Him." (Muslim)

Examples include: Dhikr after prayer in congregation, gatherings of knowledge where Allah is remembered, etc.

10. Numerical Dhikr

Many adhkar come with specific numbers recommended by the Prophet (ﷺ):

  • 3 times: Many adhkar are repeated thrice (like after prayer)
  • 7 times: Some protective adhkar
  • 10 times: Certain morning/evening adhkar
  • 33 times: Tasbih, Tahmid, Takbir after prayer
  • 100 times: Daily istighfar, SubhanAllah wa bihamdihi

    • Which Type Should You Focus On?

    The best approach is balanced:

    1. Start with obligatory: Adhkar in salah, morning/evening adhkar
    2. Add occasion-specific: Before sleep, after prayers, when eating
    3. Fill gaps with unrestricted: Use free time for general dhikr
    4. Combine tongue and heart: Always try to understand and feel what you're saying
    5. Be consistent: Better to do a little regularly than a lot occasionally

    The beauty of dhikr is that it has a form suitable for every person, every time, and every situation. Whether you're busy or free, sitting or moving, alone or with others - there's always a way to remember Allah.

    Conclusion

    Understanding the different types of dhikr helps you build a comprehensive practice that covers all aspects of your day. Don't limit yourself to just one type. Mix them, rotate them, and let the remembrance of Allah become woven into every moment of your life. As the Quran says: "So remember Me; I will remember you." (2:152)
