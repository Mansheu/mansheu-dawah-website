Community Food Drive Success

By Admin Team

Published: November 29, 2025 · Jumada al-Awwal 25, 1447

Updated: November 30, 2025 · Jumada al-Awwal 26, 1447

The Mansheu Dawah Relief Network wrapped up its annual community food drive on Saturday with delivery trucks still rolling into late afternoon, a visible sign that donations this year exceeded every target set back in September.

Coordinated from the Agege Civic Centre and twelve satellite depots, the drive drew participation from masajid, businesses, and women-led cooperatives across Lagos State. Rains threatened to slow loading lines on Friday evening, but the volunteer corps adjusted shifts and kept pallets moving until the final manifest was signed.

Drive Smashes Previous Records

Between 22 and 28 November, donors contributed 48 tonnes of rice, grains, beans, cooking oil, and protein packs - an increase of 37 percent over last year. Cash pledges worth NGN 42 million funded bulk purchases for items that could not be collected in kind, allowing organisers to assemble 2,400 family hampers.

Distribution focused on households flagged by the Mansheu Welfare Registry in Agege, Ikotun, Makoko, and Ajegunle. Each hamper contains three weeks' worth of staples plus hygiene kits, Qur'an readers, and activity cards for children emphasising shukr journals.

Volunteer Corps Keeps Operations Fluid

A total of 320 volunteers rotated through packing shifts, traffic control, warehouse documentation, and home deliveries. Logistics captain Ibrahim Danmusa reported that students from the Mansheu Technical Academy designed conveyor ramps from reclaimed wood to reduce lifting injuries.

  • 18 neighborhood collection hubs kept refrigerated storage for perishables
  • 6 medical professionals staffed a wellness tent for volunteers
  • 11 ride-hailing companies provided subsidised vans for last-mile drops
  • 24 imams announced donation tallies after Jumu'ah to sustain momentum
  • 200 youth scouts handled door-to-door mapping with GPS check-ins
  • 8 sign-language interpreters supported deaf beneficiaries at pickup points

All entries were logged in a custom Power Apps dashboard so coordinators could reconcile deliveries in real time. The data will be shared with the Lagos State Ministry of Youth and Social Development as part of a lessons-learned brief.

Families Share the Impact

Residents of Makoko's Adekunle axis welcomed teams with recitations of Surah Rahman before guiding them through the narrow boardwalks. Widowed mother Halimah Adeoye said the hamper means she can keep her twins enrolled in Qur'an school without worrying about groceries for the next fortnight.

"We plan this drive as ibadah. Every kilogram delivered represents a dua answered for someone balancing debt, school fees, and dignity. The gratitude we witnessed in Ajegunle yesterday will stay with our team all year."

Hajia Aminat Sule, Relief Network Director

Beneficiaries also completed anonymous feedback cards highlighting where the process can improve. Several suggested adding charcoal vouchers for households that still cook with outdoor stoves, a recommendation the relief team has already escalated to its procurement partners.

Forward Plans for 2026

Planning for next year's drive begins in January, with hopes to extend deliveries to two riverine communities in Epe. Discussions are underway with the International Humanitarian Logistics Association to provide cold-chain training for volunteer drivers transporting protein packs.

Until then, the relief network will continue weekly soup kitchens at the Mansheu Outreach Center and will repurpose leftover packaging materials for January's back-to-school kit distribution. Donors can still contribute via the sadaqah portal, where every NGN 5,000 pledge unlocks a mini food basket for families on the waiting list.
