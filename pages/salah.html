Essential Salah Du'as

  1. Home
  2. Dhikr & Du'a
  3. Salah Du'as

Prayer (Salah) is the cornerstone of Islamic worship, and within it lie beautiful supplications that connect the heart directly to Allah. These essential du'as enhance our prayer experience and draw us closer to our Creator.

The Prophet ﷺ taught us specific supplications to recite during different parts of the prayer, each carrying profound meaning and spiritual benefit. Learning these du'as enriches our Salah and deepens our connection with Allah.

These authentic supplications from the Quran and Sunnah help us achieve khushoo (humility and concentration) in prayer and make our Salah more meaningful and spiritually fulfilling.

Du'a al-Istiftah (Opening Supplication)

سُبْحَانَكَ اللَّهُمَّ وَبِحَمْدِكَ، وَتَبَارَكَ اسْمُكَ، وَتَعَالَى جَدُّكَ، وَلَا إِلَهَ غَيْرُكَ

(After Takbeer al-Ihram)

Glory be to You, O Allah, and praise be to You. Blessed is Your Name and exalted is Your Majesty. There is no deity worthy of worship except You.

Subḥānaka-llāhumma wa bi-ḥamdika, wa tabāraka-smuka, wa taʿālā jadduka, wa lā ilāha ghayruka.

The Prophetic Opening

Abū Hurayrah (raḍiy Allāhu ʿanhu) reported that when the Messenger of Allah ﷺ stood up for prayer, he would remain silent for a moment after the takbīr. When asked about this, he ﷺ said: "I say: 'Subḥānaka-llāhumma wa bi-ḥamdika, wa tabāraka-smuka, wa taʿālā jadduka, wa lā ilāha ghayruka.'" (Abū Dāwūd, Tirmidhī)

Purification of Intent

This opening du'ā serves as a spiritual preparation, purifying the heart and mind before standing before Allah. It acknowledges Allah's perfect attributes and creates the proper mindset for worship.

ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb (raḍiy Allāhu ʿanhu) used to recite this du'ā aloud so that people could hear and learn it, showing its importance in establishing the correct beginning of prayer.

Praise and Glory to Allah

• "Subḥānaka" - Glorifying Allah, declaring Him free from any imperfection

• "Bi-ḥamdika" - Acknowledging that all praise belongs to Allah alone

• Combines tasbīḥ (glorification) and ḥamd (praise) in perfect harmony

Blessed and Exalted Names

• "Tabāraka-smuka" - Allah's Name is blessed and brings blessings

• Every Name of Allah contains infinite blessing and benefit

• "Taʿālā jadduka" - Allah's Majesty is exalted above all creation

• "Jadd" refers to Allah's greatness, magnificence, and bounty

Absolute Monotheism

• "Lā ilāha ghayruka" - Declaring Allah as the only deity

• Purifies the heart from any form of shirk (associating partners)

• Establishes complete focus on Allah alone during prayer

Spiritual Preparation

• Creates the proper spiritual state for prayer

• Helps achieve khushūʿ (humility and concentration)

• Transitions from worldly concerns to divine worship

• Recommended to recite silently after takbīr al-iḥrām

Seeking Refuge Before Quran Recitation

أَعُوذُ بِاللَّهِ مِنَ الشَّيْطَانِ الرَّجِيمِ

(Before reciting Quran in prayer)

I seek refuge with Allah from Satan, the accursed.

Aʿūdhu billāhi mina-sh-shayṭāni-r-rajīm.

Divine Command for Protection

Allah commands in the Quran: "So when you recite the Quran, [first] seek refuge in Allah from Satan, the expelled [from His mercy]." (An-Nahl 16:98)

This verse makes seeking refuge with Allah a requirement before reciting the Quran, whether in prayer or outside of it.

Prophetic Practice

The Prophet ﷺ consistently sought refuge with Allah before reciting the Quran in prayer, teaching us the importance of spiritual protection during this sacred act of worship.

Ibn Masʿūd (raḍiy Allāhu ʿanhu) reported that seeking refuge should be done before reciting al-Fātiḥah in prayer, creating a barrier against satanic whispers.

Seeking Divine Protection

• "Aʿūdhu" - seeking refuge, protection, and safety with Allah

• Acknowledges our complete dependence on Allah's protection

• Shows humility before approaching the sacred words of Allah

Protection from Satan

• Satan tries to distract during Quran recitation and prayer

• "Ar-Rajīm" - the expelled, banished from Allah's mercy

• Creates spiritual barrier against whispers and distractions

• Purifies the heart to receive the Quran's guidance

Quranic Commandment

• Direct command from Allah in Sūrah an-Naḥl

• Shows the importance Allah places on pure recitation

• Applies to all Quran recitation, not just in prayer

Practical Application

• Recite silently before beginning Sūrah al-Fātiḥah

• Can be recited once per prayer or before each recitation

• Helps achieve better concentration and presence of heart

• Essential for protecting the spiritual benefits of Quran recitation

Du'a al-Qunoot (Supplication of Standing)

اللَّهُمَّ اهْدِنِي فِيمَنْ هَدَيْتَ، وَعَافِنِي فِيمَنْ عَافَيْتَ، وَتَوَلَّنِي فِيمَنْ تَوَلَّيْتَ، وَبَارِكْ لِي فِيمَا أَعْطَيْتَ، وَقِنِي شَرَّ مَا قَضَيْتَ، فَإِنَّكَ تَقْضِي وَلَا يُقْضَى عَلَيْكَ، إِنَّهُ لَا يَذِلُّ مَنْ وَالَيْتَ، وَلَا يَعِزُّ مَنْ عَادَيْتَ، تَبَارَكْتَ رَبَّنَا وَتَعَالَيْتَ

(In Witr prayer and during calamities)

O Allah, guide me among those You have guided, grant me safety among those You have granted safety, take me into Your care among those You have taken into Your care, bless me in what You have given me, and protect me from the evil of what You have decreed. Indeed, You decree and none can decree against You. None can be humiliated whom You have befriended, and none can be honored whom You have taken as an enemy. Blessed are You, our Lord, and exalted.

Allāhumma-hdinī fī-man hadayta, wa ʿāfinī fī-man ʿāfayta, wa tawallanī fī-man tawallayta, wa bārik lī fī-mā aʿṭayta, wa qinī sharra mā qaḍayta, fa-innaka taqḍī wa lā yuqḍā ʿalayka, innahu lā yadhillu man wālayta, wa lā yaʿizzu man ʿādayta, tabārakta Rabbanā wa taʿālayta.

The Prophet's Regular Du'a

Al-Ḥasan ibn ʿAlī (raḍiy Allāhu ʿanhumā) said: "The Messenger of Allah ﷺ taught me words to say during the qunūt of witr: 'Allāhumma-hdinī fī-man hadayta...' (this du'ā)." (Abū Dāwūd, Tirmidhī, Nasā'ī, Ibn Mājah)

Comprehensive Supplication

This du'ā covers all essential needs: guidance, safety, divine care, blessings in provisions, and protection from decreed trials. It acknowledges Allah's absolute authority while seeking His mercy.

Times of Recitation

• Primarily recited during the qunūt of witr prayer

• Also recommended during times of calamity and hardship

• Can be recited in any prayer when seeking Allah's guidance and protection

Five Essential Requests

• Guidance: "Ahdini" - seeking right path and correct decisions

• Safety: "ʿĀfini" - protection from all forms of harm

• Divine Care: "Tawallanī" - Allah taking charge of our affairs

• Blessed Provisions: "Bārik lī" - seeking barakah in what Allah gives

• Protection from Trials: "Qinī sharra" - safety from decreed difficulties

Acknowledging Divine Authority

• "Taqḍī wa lā yuqḍā ʿalayka" - Allah's decree is absolute

• Shows complete submission to Allah's wisdom and timing

• Recognizes that all affairs are under Allah's control

Divine Friendship vs Enmity

• "Lā yadhillu man wālayta" - Allah's friends cannot be truly humiliated

• "Lā yaʿizzu man ʿādayta" - Allah's enemies cannot achieve true honor

• Emphasizes the ultimate importance of Allah's pleasure

Praise and Exaltation

• "Tabārakta Rabbanā wa taʿālayta" - blessing and exalting Allah

• Ends with praise, showing proper etiquette of du'ā

• Acknowledges Allah's blessed and exalted nature

At-Tashahhud (The Testimony in Final Sitting)

التَّحِيَّاتُ لِلَّهِ، وَالصَّلَوَاتُ وَالطَّيِّبَاتُ، السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكَ أَيُّهَا النَّبِيُّ وَرَحْمَةُ اللَّهِ وَبَرَكَاتُهُ، السَّلَامُ عَلَيْنَا وَعَلَى عِبَادِ اللَّهِ الصَّالِحِينَ، أَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ، وَأَشْهَدُ أَنَّ مُحَمَّداً عَبْدُهُ وَرَسُولُهُ

(In the final sitting of every prayer)

All greetings, prayers, and good things belong to Allah. Peace be upon you, O Prophet, and Allah's mercy and His blessings. Peace be upon us and upon the righteous servants of Allah. I bear witness that there is no deity except Allah, and I bear witness that Muhammad is His servant and messenger.

At-taḥiyyātu lillāhi, wa-ṣ-ṣalawātu wa-ṭ-ṭayyibāt. As-salāmu ʿalayka ayyuha-n-nabiyyu wa raḥmatu-llāhi wa barakātuh. As-salāmu ʿalaynā wa ʿalā ʿibādi-llāhi-ṣ-ṣāliḥīn. Ashhadu an lā ilāha illa-llāh, wa ashhadu anna Muḥammadan ʿabduhu wa rasūluh.

The Conversation Between Allah and His Prophet

Ibn Masʿūd (raḍiy Allāhu ʿanhu) said: "The Messenger of Allah ﷺ taught us the tashahhud as he taught us a chapter of the Quran." He then recited this exact formulation. (Bukhārī, Muslim)

A Divine Dialogue

Some scholars explain that the tashahhud represents the conversation that took place during the Prophet's night journey (Isrā' and Miʿrāj). When the Prophet ﷺ met Allah, he said: "At-taḥiyyātu lillāh" (All greetings belong to Allah). Allah responded with peace and blessings upon the Prophet, and the Prophet extended peace to all righteous servants.

Essential Component of Prayer

The tashahhud is a pillar (rukn) of prayer according to most scholars. It must be recited in every prayer, and forgetting it requires the prostration of forgetfulness (sujūd as-sahw).

Acknowledgment of Allah's Sovereignty

• "At-taḥiyyātu lillāh" - All forms of greeting and honor belong to Allah

• "Aṣ-ṣalawātu" - All prayers and acts of worship are for Allah

• "Aṭ-ṭayyibātu" - All good words and deeds are dedicated to Allah

Greeting the Prophet ﷺ

• Direct address to the Prophet with respect and honor

• Seeking Allah's mercy and blessings upon him

• Shows the special status of the Prophet in our prayers

• Creates spiritual connection across time and space

Universal Peace

• "ʿAlaynā" - including ourselves in the prayer for peace

• "ʿIbādi-llāhi-ṣ-ṣāliḥīn" - extending peace to all righteous servants

• Creates unity among believers worldwide

• Angels say "Āmīn" when this is recited sincerely

Declaration of Faith

• Shahādah (testimony of faith) concludes the tashahhud

• Reaffirms Islamic monotheism and Muhammad's messengership

• Makes prayer a renewal of one's Islamic commitment

• Links belief to worship in every prayer
