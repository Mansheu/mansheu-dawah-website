After Salah Adhkar

  1. Home
  2. Dhikr & Du'a
  3. After Salah Adhkar

After completing each prayer, the Prophet ﷺ taught us specific remembrances that multiply the rewards of our Salah and maintain our connection with Allah. These post-prayer adhkar are among the most beloved acts to Allah.

The moments following prayer are precious opportunities for dhikr, du'a, and reflection. The Prophet ﷺ never left the prayer mat without engaging in these beautiful remembrances that purify the heart and strengthen faith.

Regular recitation of after-salah adhkar brings immense spiritual benefits, protects from harm, and serves as a bridge between the formal prayer and our daily activities, keeping us mindful of Allah throughout the day.

Istighfar - Seeking Forgiveness

أَسْتَغْفِرُ اللَّهَ، أَسْتَغْفِرُ اللَّهَ، أَسْتَغْفِرُ اللَّهَ

(3x after each prayer)

I seek forgiveness from Allah. I seek forgiveness from Allah. I seek forgiveness from Allah.

Astaghfiru-llāh, Astaghfiru-llāh, Astaghfiru-llāh.

The First Act After Prayer

Thawbān (raḍiy Allāhu ʿanhu) reported: "When the Messenger of Allah ﷺ finished his prayer, he would seek forgiveness three times and then say: 'Allāhumma anta-s-Salāmu wa minka-s-salām, tabārakta yā dhā-l-jalāli wa-l-ikrām.'" (Muslim 591)

Acknowledging Human Imperfection

Even after completing the act of worship, the Prophet ﷺ immediately sought Allah's forgiveness, teaching us humility and recognition that our worship may contain shortcomings or distractions.

This practice acknowledges that no human worship is perfect and that we constantly need Allah's forgiveness and mercy, even for our acts of obedience.

Immediate Purification

• Seeks forgiveness for any deficiencies in the prayer just completed

• Acknowledges that human worship is never perfect

• Shows humility before Allah despite completing an act of worship

Triple Emphasis

• Recited three times for emphasis and completeness

• Each repetition increases sincerity and focus

• Number three carries special significance in Islamic practices

Spiritual Benefits

• Purifies the prayer from any shortcomings

• Maintains humility and God-consciousness

• Opens the door for Allah's forgiveness and acceptance

• Prepares the heart for the subsequent dhikr

The Triple Glorification

سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ (٣٣)
الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ (٣٣)
اللَّهُ أَكْبَرُ (٣٤)

(Total: 100 after each prayer)

SubḥānAllāh (33 times): Glory be to Allah
Al-ḥamdu lillāh (33 times): All praise is for Allah
Allāhu akbar (34 times): Allah is the Greatest

SubḥānAllāh (33 times)
Al-ḥamdu lillāh (33 times)
Allāhu akbar (34 times)

Forgiveness of Sins Like Ocean Foam

Abū Hurayrah (raḍiy Allāhu ʿanhu) reported that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Whoever glorifies Allah (SubḥānAllāh) thirty-three times immediately after each prayer, and praises Allah (Al-ḥamdu lillāh) thirty-three times, and magnifies Allah (Allāhu akbar) thirty-four times, so there are ninety-nine, and to complete one hundred says: 'Lā ilāha illa-llāhu waḥdahu lā sharīka lah, lahu-l-mulku wa lahu-l-ḥamd, wa huwa ʿalā kulli shay'in qadīr' – his sins will be forgiven even if they were like the foam of the sea." (Muslim 597)

Easy Yet Mighty Reward

The Prophet ﷺ taught that these simple words, when recited after each prayer, carry enormous spiritual weight and can erase a lifetime of sins. The ease of the practice contrasts with the magnitude of its reward.

Three Pillars of Praise

• SubḥānAllāh: Declares Allah free from all imperfections

• Al-ḥamdu lillāh: Acknowledges Allah as the source of all good

• Allāhu akbar: Affirms Allah's supremacy over all creation

Perfect Distribution

• 33 + 33 + 34 = 100 total remembrances

• Can be counted on fingers or with prayer beads (tasbih)

• The final "Allāhu akbar" gets the extra count, emphasizing Allah's greatness

Spiritual Transformation

• Transitions the mind from prayer back to worldly activities

• Maintains consciousness of Allah after formal worship

• Creates a spiritual shield through constant remembrance

Practical Benefits

• Simple enough to memorize and practice consistently

• Can be done anywhere after prayer

• Builds a habit of regular dhikr throughout the day

Ayat al-Kursi - The Ultimate Protection

اللَّهُ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ الْحَيُّ الْقَيُّومُ ۚ لَا تَأْخُذُهُ سِنَةٌ وَلَا نَوْمٌ ۚ لَهُ مَا فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَمَا فِي الْأَرْضِ ۗ مَنْ ذَا الَّذِي يَشْفَعُ عِنْدَهُ إِلَّا بِإِذْنِهِ ۚ يَعْلَمُ مَا بَيْنَ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَمَا خَلْفَهُمْ ۖ وَلَا يُحِيطُونَ بِشَيْءٍ مِنْ عِلْمِهِ إِلَّا بِمَا شَاءَ ۚ وَسِعَ كُرْسِيُّهُ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ ۖ وَلَا يَئُودُهُ حِفْظُهُمَا ۚ وَهُوَ الْعَلِيُّ الْعَظِيمُ

(Once after each prayer)

Allah - there is no deity except Him, the Ever-Living, the Self-Sustaining. Neither drowsiness overtakes Him nor sleep. To Him belongs whatever is in the heavens and whatever is on the earth. Who is it that can intercede with Him except by His permission? He knows what is before them and what will be after them, and they encompass not a thing of His knowledge except for what He wills. His Kursi extends over the heavens and the earth, and their preservation tires Him not. And He is the Most High, the Most Great.

Allāhu lā ilāha illā Huwa, al-Ḥayyu-l-Qayyūm. Lā ta'khudhuhu sinatun wa lā nawm. Lahu mā fī-s-samāwāti wa mā fī-l-arḍ. Man dhā-lladhī yashfaʿu ʿindahu illā bi-idhnī. Yaʿlamu mā bayna aydīhim wa mā khalfahum, wa lā yuḥīṭūna bi-shay'in min ʿilmihi illā bi-mā shā'. Wasiʿa kursiyyuhu-s-samāwāti wa-l-arḍ, wa lā ya'ūduhu ḥifẓuhumā, wa Huwa-l-ʿAliyyu-l-ʿAẓīm.

Direct Path to Paradise

Abū Umāmah (raḍiy Allāhu ʿanhu) reported that the Prophet ﷺ said: "Whoever recites Āyat al-Kursī immediately after each compulsory prayer, there will be nothing between him and entering Paradise except death." (Nasā'ī, Ibn Ḥibbān)

The Greatest Verse

The Prophet ﷺ asked Ubayy ibn Kaʿb (raḍiy Allāhu ʿanhu): "Which verse in Allah's Book is the greatest?" When he replied "Āyat al-Kursī," the Prophet ﷺ struck him on the chest and said: "May knowledge bring you joy, O Abū Mundhir!" (Muslim 810)

Protection Until the Next Prayer

Reciting Āyat al-Kursī after each prayer provides divine protection until the next prayer, creating a continuous shield throughout the day and night.

Divine Attributes in Perfect Harmony

• Al-Ḥayy (Ever-Living) - Allah possesses perfect, eternal life

• Al-Qayyūm (Self-Sustaining) - Allah maintains all existence

• Perfect knowledge encompassing past, present, and future

• Absolute sovereignty over all creation

Comprehensive Protection

• Creates spiritual barrier against all forms of harm

• Invokes Allah's most powerful attributes for protection

• Guaranteed divine care until the next prayer

Bridge to Paradise

• Regular recitation after each prayer leads to Paradise

• Demonstrates commitment to consistent worship

• Strengthens connection with Allah's greatest attributes

Daily Spiritual Routine

• Recited five times daily after obligatory prayers

• Creates rhythm of divine remembrance throughout day

• Maintains high spiritual state between prayers
