The Qur'an: The Best Dhikr

5 min read

The Supreme Form of Remembrance

The Qur'an is the speech of Allah (سبحانه وتعالى) and represents the highest form of dhikr (remembrance). When we recite the Qur'an, we are engaging directly with Allah's words, and this brings us closer to Him in a way that no other form of remembrance can match.

Why the Qur'an is the Best Dhikr

The Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ) said: "The best dhikr is the recitation of the Qur'an." This is because:

  • It is Allah's Direct Speech: Unlike other forms of dhikr which are our words directed to Allah, the Qur'an is Allah's words to us
  • Comprehensive Guidance: It contains everything we need for spiritual growth, moral guidance, and worldly success
  • Multiplied Rewards: Each letter of the Qur'an earns ten rewards, and there is no limit to how many letters one can recite
  • Intercessor on the Day of Judgment: The Qur'an will intercede for those who recited it regularly

The Virtues of Qur'anic Recitation

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "Whoever recites a letter from the Book of Allah, he will receive one good deed as ten good deeds like it. I do not say that Alif Lam Mim is one letter, but rather Alif is a letter, Lam is a letter, and Mim is a letter." (Tirmidhi)

How to Make the Qur'an Part of Your Daily Dhikr

  1. Set a Daily Goal: Start with a small, achievable target (e.g., one page or 5 minutes daily)
  2. Choose a Consistent Time: Morning after Fajr or evening after Maghrib are blessed times
  3. Recite with Tajweed: Learn the proper pronunciation to honor Allah's words
  4. Reflect on the Meanings: Read the translation to understand what Allah is saying to you
  5. Memorize Gradually: Start with short surahs and build your memorization over time

The Spiritual Impact

Regular recitation of the Qur'an transforms the heart. It brings:

  • Inner peace and tranquility
  • Increased faith and connection with Allah
  • Protection from shaytan and evil
  • Light in the heart and clarity in decision-making
  • Healing for both physical and spiritual ailments

الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَتَطْمَئِنُّ قُلُوبُهُم بِذِكْرِ اللَّهِ ۗ أَلَا بِذِكْرِ اللَّهِ تَطْمَئِنُّ الْقُلُوبُ

"Those who have believed and whose hearts are assured by the remembrance of Allah. Unquestionably, by the remembrance of Allah hearts are assured." - Qur'an 13:28

Practical Tips for Busy Schedules

  • Listen to Qur'an during your commute
  • Use spare moments to recite short surahs you've memorized
  • Join a Qur'an study circle to stay motivated
  • Use apps to track your daily reading progress
  • Teach your family, especially children, to love the Qur'an

Remember

The Qur'an is not just a book to be read—it is a conversation with Allah. When you recite it, Allah is speaking to you. When you reflect upon it, you are contemplating His wisdom. When you act upon it, you are living in accordance with His will. Make the Qur'an your constant companion, and you will find peace, guidance, and success in this life and the next.
