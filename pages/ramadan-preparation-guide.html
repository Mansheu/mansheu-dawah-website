Ramadan Preparation Guide Released

By Editorial Team

Published: December 2, 2025 · Jumada al-Awwal 28, 1447

Updated: December 3, 2025 · Jumada al-Awwal 29, 1447

A comprehensive 40-page Ramadan Preparation Guide released on Tuesday by Mansheu Dawah's Community Learning Desk stitches together prayer timetables, nutrition plans, worship challenges, and service rosters to help households across Lagos enter the blessed month with clarity.

Within 24 hours of its publication, 5,200 families downloaded the document, while 1,000 printed copies were dispatched to Agege, Surulere, and Ikorodu study hubs for members who prefer an offline reference. Coordinators say the guide consolidates lessons from last year's pilot and responds to feedback gathered during forty listening circles held in October.

Guide Aligns Homes and Masajid

The Ramadan Preparedness Taskforce, chaired by Imam Musa Abiola of Al-Hikmah Mosque, structured the guide around a "household-to-masjid" framework. Each chapter matches domestic routines with communal programming so that suhoor, school runs, office schedules, and nightly prayers stay in sync with the masjid's agenda.

Planning worksheets encourage families to note the closest women's halaqah, youth khatm circle, and zakat disbursement point. "We realised residents were toggling between multiple WhatsApp groups, so this single document now provides a master map for the entire month," Imam Abiola said during the virtual launch webinar.

Schedules Tailored for Lagos Households

A data team led by volunteer analyst Hafsah Raji modelled sunrise and sunset times for all Lagos coastal and mainland zones, producing hyperlocal schedules. Sample routines demonstrate how nurses on night duty, university students writing finals, and market women who commute before dawn can adapt fasting hours without burnout.

  • Zonal suhoor and iftar alerts aligned with Lekki, Agege, Ikorodu, and Badagry time differentials
  • Family worship maps pairing taraweeh venues with parking and child-minding options
  • A 30-day Qur'an reading ladder with companion tafsir podcasts
  • Nutrition plans using affordable staples such as ofada rice, millet pap, and plantain flour
  • Giving Circles calendar highlighting weekly sadaqah projects and zakat clinics
  • Youth volunteer shifts tied to community iftars and sanitation brigades

The document also lists 18 partner mosques prepared to host respite corners for breastfeeding mothers and diabetic congregants. Each site shares phone numbers of on-call medics trained to advise on safe fasting protocols.

Digital Toolkit and Mentors

Alongside the PDF, a lightweight progressive web app now houses editable versions of the planner, a zakat calculator, and a tracker that nudges users when they miss a day's Qur'an quota. The tech stack was built by the Mansheu Labs Fellowship and integrates with the existing digital tasbih counter launched earlier this year.

Forty-two volunteer mentors have been assigned to WhatsApp cohorts of 25 families each, offering weekly Q&A sessions on fiqh rulings for travellers, nutrition for expecting mothers, and mental health resources. Each mentor keeps anonymised progress reports so that the taskforce can measure adoption and iterate quickly.

Community Voices and Implementation

Area councils began distributing the printed versions after Jumu'ah last week, while youth leaders scheduled three roadshows in Mushin, Ajah, and Idimu to demonstrate how to fill out the planner. Several madrasah principals have already uploaded the guide to their LMS portals and will hold assemblies next week to familiarise students with the recommendations.

"When families see their exact neighbourhood, masjid, and volunteer lane referenced, they realise Ramadan preparation is not abstract. This guide proves we have mapped their realities into an achievable worship plan."

Maryam Ajoke, Programs Coordinator

Registration is open for the final pre-Ramadan orientation slated for January 11 at the Mansheu Knowledge Campus, where facilitators will walk through the guide chapter by chapter. The taskforce plans to release an update in Sha'ban reflecting moon-sighting guidance from the Supreme Council for Islamic Affairs.
