Ruqyah & Healing

  1. Home
  2. Dhikr & Du'a
  3. Ruqyah & Healing

Ruqyah is the Islamic practice of seeking Allah's protection and healing through recitation of Quranic verses and prophetic supplications. It serves as a means of spiritual and physical healing, relying entirely on Allah's power and mercy.

The Prophet ﷺ practiced and endorsed Ruqyah as a legitimate form of seeking Allah's cure from various ailments, evil eye, witchcraft, and spiritual afflictions. These authentic supplications provide comfort and healing when recited with sincere faith and trust in Allah.

Regular recitation of these protective verses strengthens one's spiritual immunity, brings peace to the heart, and serves as a shield against various forms of harm. They are particularly beneficial during times of illness, distress, or spiritual difficulty.

Surah al-Fatihah - The Opening (Complete Healing)

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ ۝ الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ ۝ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ ۝ مَالِكِ يَوْمِ الدِّينِ ۝ إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ وَإِيَّاكَ نَسْتَعِينُ ۝ اهْدِنَا الصِّرَاطَ الْمُسْتَقِيمَ ۝ صِرَاطَ الَّذِينَ أَنْعَمْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ غَيْرِ الْمَغْضُوبِ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَا الضَّالِّينَ

(7x for Ruqyah)

In the name of Allah, the Entirely Merciful, the Especially Merciful. [All] praise is [due] to Allah, Lord of the worlds - The Entirely Merciful, the Especially Merciful, Sovereign of the Day of Recompense. It is You we worship and You we ask for help. Guide us to the straight path - The path of those upon whom You have bestowed favor, not of those who have evoked [Your] anger or of those who are astray.

Bismillāhi-r-raḥmāni-r-raḥīm. Al-ḥamdu lillāhi rabbi-l-ʿālamīn. Ar-raḥmāni-r-raḥīm. Māliki yawmi-d-dīn. Iyyāka naʿbudu wa iyyāka nastaʿīn. Ihdinā-ṣ-ṣirāṭa-l-mustaqīm. Ṣirāṭa-lladhīna anʿamta ʿalayhim ghayri-l-maghḍūbi ʿalayhim wa lā-ḍ-ḍāllīn.

The Greatest Ruqyah

Abū Saʿīd al-Khudrī (raḍiy Allāhu ʿanhu) reported that some of the companions of the Prophet ﷺ came upon a tribe among the Arab tribes, and that tribe did not show them hospitality. While they were in that state, the chief of that tribe was bitten by a snake (or stung by a scorpion). They said: "Have you got any medicine or anyone who can treat with Ruqyah?" The companions said: "You refuse to show us hospitality, and we will not perform Ruqyah for him unless you pay us for it." So they agreed to pay them a flock of sheep. One of them (companions) started reciting Sūrah al-Fātiḥah and gathering his saliva and spitting it out (on the bite). The chief got cured as if he was released from a chain, and his people paid them what they agreed to pay. When they returned to the Prophet ﷺ and informed him, he smiled and said: "How did you come to know that Sūrah al-Fātiḥah was a Ruqyah?" (Bukhārī 5736)

Complete Healing Power

This incident demonstrates the incredible healing power contained in Sūrah al-Fātiḥah. The Prophet ﷺ confirmed its effectiveness as Ruqyah, establishing it as the most comprehensive healing supplication in Islam.

Comprehensive Healing Elements

• Bismillāh: Invoking Allah's mercy and blessing for healing

• Ar-Raḥmān, Ar-Raḥīm: Calling upon Allah's infinite mercy

• Rabb al-ʿĀlamīn: The Lord and Sustainer who controls all healing

• Mālik yawm ad-Dīn: Master of the Day of Judgment, ultimate authority

Exclusive Worship and Dependence

• "Iyyāka naʿbudu" - We worship You alone (rejecting false healers)

• "Iyyāka nastaʿīn" - We seek help from You alone

• Establishes complete reliance on Allah for healing

Guidance and Protection

• Seeking the straight path away from misguidance

• Following the path of those blessed by Allah

• Avoiding paths that lead to spiritual and physical harm

Ruqyah Practice

• Recite 7 times for maximum effect

• Can be recited over water to drink or use for washing

• Place hand on area of pain/illness while reciting

• Blow gently after recitation (as per Prophetic practice)

Al-Mu'awwidhatayn - The Two Seeking Refuge (Surah al-Falaq & an-Nas)

سُورَةُ الْفَلَقِ:
بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ ۝ قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ الْفَلَقِ ۝ مِنْ شَرِّ مَا خَلَقَ ۝ وَمِنْ شَرِّ غَاسِقٍ إِذَا وَقَبَ ۝ وَمِنْ شَرِّ النَّفَّاثَاتِ فِي الْعُقَدِ ۝ وَمِنْ شَرِّ حَاسِدٍ إِذَا حَسَدَ

سُورَةُ النَّاسِ:
بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ ۝ قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ النَّاسِ ۝ مَلِكِ النَّاسِ ۝ إِلَٰهِ النَّاسِ ۝ مِنْ شَرِّ الْوَسْوَاسِ الْخَنَّاسِ ۝ الَّذِي يُوَسْوِسُ فِي صُدُورِ النَّاسِ ۝ مِنَ الْجِنَّةِ وَالنَّاسِ

(3x each for protection)

Surah al-Falaq: Say: "I seek refuge with the Lord of the dawn from the evil of what He created and from the evil of darkness when it settles and from the evil of the blowers in knots and from the evil of an envier when he envies."

Surah an-Nas: Say: "I seek refuge with the Lord of mankind, The King of mankind, The God of mankind, from the evil of the retreating whisperer who whispers [evil] into the breasts of mankind - from among the jinn and mankind."

Surah al-Falaq: Qul aʿūdhu bi-rabbi-l-falaq. Min sharri mā khalaq. Wa min sharri ghāsiqin idhā waqab. Wa min sharri-n-naffāthāti fī-l-ʿuqad. Wa min sharri ḥāsidin idhā ḥasad.

Surah an-Nas: Qul aʿūdhu bi-rabbi-n-nās. Maliki-n-nās. Ilāhi-n-nās. Min sharri-l-waswāsi-l-khannās. Alladhī yuwaswisu fī ṣudūri-n-nās. Mina-l-jinnati wa-n-nās.

The Prophet's Ultimate Protection

ʿĀ'ishah (raḍiy Allāhu ʿanhā) reported: "When the Prophet ﷺ went to bed every night, he would hold his palms together and blow into them, recite Sūrah al-Ikhlāṣ, Sūrah al-Falaq and Sūrah an-Nās, and then wipe over his body as much as he could reach, beginning with his head and face and the front of his body. He would do this three times." (Bukhārī 5017)

Protection from Magic and Evil Eye

These two chapters specifically address protection from witchcraft, evil eye, envy, and satanic whispers. They form a complete shield against both seen and unseen forms of harm.

Comprehensive Divine Refuge

ʿUqbah ibn ʿĀmir (raḍiy Allāhu ʿanhu) reported that the Prophet ﷺ said: "Do you not see that verses have been revealed to me tonight the like of which has never been seen: 'Say: I seek refuge with the Lord of the dawn' and 'Say: I seek refuge with the Lord of mankind.'" (Muslim 814)

Surah al-Falaq - External Protection

• Rabb al-Falaq: Lord of the dawn, dispelling all darkness

• Sharr mā khalaq: Evil of all created beings

• Ghāsiq idhā waqab: Darkness and its harmful effects

• Naffāthāt fī al-ʿuqad: Witchcraft and magic

• Ḥāsid idhā ḥasad: Envy and evil eye

Surah an-Nas - Internal Protection

• Rabb an-Nās: Lord of all mankind

• Malik an-Nās: King and Sovereign of people

• Ilāh an-Nās: God worthy of worship by all

• Waswās al-Khannās: Satanic whispers that withdraw when Allah is remembered

Complete Spiritual Protection

• Covers both external harm (al-Falaq) and internal corruption (an-Nās)

• Protects from jinn and human evil influences

• Creates impenetrable spiritual barrier when recited with faith

Ruqyah Application

• Recite 3 times each morning and evening

• Blow into cupped hands and wipe over body

• Especially effective for children and those suffering from evil eye

• Can be recited over water for drinking or bathing

Prophetic Du'a for Healing

اللَّهُمَّ رَبَّ النَّاسِ، أَذْهِبِ الْبَاسَ، اشْفِ وَأَنْتَ الشَّافِي، لَا شِفَاءَ إِلَّا شِفَاؤُكَ، شِفَاءً لَا يُغَادِرُ سَقَماً

(3x while placing hand on affected area)

O Allah, Lord of mankind, remove the hardship and heal, for You are the Healer. There is no healing except Your healing, a healing that leaves behind no ailment.

Allāhumma Rabba-n-nās, adhhib al-ba's, ishfi wa anta-sh-Shāfī, lā shifā'a illā shifā'uk, shifā'an lā yughādiru saqamā.

The Prophet's Personal Healing Du'a

ʿĀ'ishah (raḍiy Allāhu ʿanhā) reported that when the Prophet ﷺ visited any member of his family suffering from illness, he would rub the place of ailment with his right hand and say this du'ā. (Bukhārī 5675, Muslim 2191)

Complete and Perfect Healing

This du'ā specifically asks for complete healing that leaves no trace of illness behind. It acknowledges Allah as the ultimate and only true Healer, making all medical treatment merely means through which Allah grants cure.

Physical Contact and Spiritual Healing

The Prophet ﷺ would place his hand on the affected area while making this du'ā, combining physical comfort with spiritual healing, showing the holistic nature of Islamic treatment.

Addressing Allah by His Lordship

• "Rabba-n-nās" - O Lord of all mankind

• Appeals to Allah's universal care and sovereignty

• Acknowledges Allah's complete authority over human health

Requesting Relief and Healing

• "Adhhib al-ba's" - Remove the hardship/suffering

• "Ishfi" - Grant healing/cure

• Direct and simple request for divine intervention

Exclusive Divine Healing

• "Anta-sh-Shāfī" - You are THE Healer

• "Lā shifā'a illā shifā'uk" - No healing except Your healing

• Rejects dependence on anything other than Allah for true cure

Complete Restoration

• "Shifā'an lā yughādiru saqamā" - Healing that leaves no illness

• Asks for perfect, complete restoration to health

• Shows trust in Allah's power to cure completely

Method of Application

• Place right hand gently on area of pain or illness

• Recite with full concentration and faith

• Can be used for oneself or others

• Combine with seeking appropriate medical treatment
