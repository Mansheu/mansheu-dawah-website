Istighfar - Seeking Forgiveness

  1. Home
  2. Dhikr & Du'a
  3. Istighfar

Seeking Allah's forgiveness (Istighfar) is one of the most beloved acts of worship that purifies the heart, removes sins, and brings countless blessings. The Prophet ﷺ sought forgiveness more than 70 times daily despite being sinless.

Allah loves when His servants turn to Him in repentance and seek His forgiveness. These authentic forms of Istighfar from the Quran and Sunnah open doors of mercy, increase provisions, and grant relief from difficulties.

Regular Istighfar is a means of spiritual purification, increased barakah in life, and protection from trials. It demonstrates humility before Allah and acknowledgment of our constant need for His mercy and forgiveness.

Sayyid al-Istighfar - The Master of Seeking Forgiveness

اللَّهُمَّ أَنْتَ رَبِّي لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ، خَلَقْتَنِي وَأَنَا عَبْدُكَ، وَأَنَا عَلَى عَهْدِكَ وَوَعْدِكَ مَا اسْتَطَعْتُ، أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ شَرِّ مَا صَنَعْتُ، أَبُوءُ لَكَ بِنِعْمَتِكَ عَلَيَّ، وَأَبُوءُ بِذَنْبِي فَاغْفِرْ لِي فَإِنَّهُ لَا يَغْفِرُ الذُّنُوبَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ

(Morning and evening)

O Allah, You are my Lord, there is no deity except You. You created me and I am Your servant, and I abide by Your covenant and promise as best I can. I seek refuge with You from the evil of what I have done. I acknowledge Your favor upon me and I acknowledge my sin, so forgive me, for verily none can forgive sins except You.

Allāhumma anta Rabbī lā ilāha illā ant, khalaqtanī wa ana ʿabduk, wa ana ʿalā ʿahdika wa waʿdika mā-staṭaʿt, aʿūdhu bika min sharri mā ṣanaʿt, abū'u laka bi-niʿmatika ʿalayy, wa abū'u bi-dhanbī fa-ghfir lī fa-innahu lā yaghfiru-dh-dhunūba illā ant.

Guarantee of Paradise

Shaddād ibn Aws (raḍiy Allāhu ʿanhu) reported that the Prophet ﷺ said: "The master of seeking forgiveness is that the servant says: 'Allāhumma anta Rabbī...' (this du'ā). Whoever says it during the day with firm faith and dies on that day before evening, he will be among the people of Paradise. And whoever says it at night with firm faith and dies before morning, he will be among the people of Paradise." (Bukhārī 6306)

The Most Comprehensive Istighfar

This du'ā is called "Sayyid al-Istighfar" (the master of seeking forgiveness) because it contains all elements of perfect repentance: acknowledging Allah's lordship, confessing servitude, seeking refuge from evil deeds, recognizing Allah's blessings, and admitting one's sins.

Perfect Elements of Repentance

• Recognition of Allah's Lordship: "Anta Rabbī lā ilāha illā ant"

• Acknowledging Creation and Servitude: "Khalaqtanī wa ana ʿabduk"

• Commitment to Covenant: "ʿAlā ʿahdika wa waʿdika mā-staṭaʿt"

• Seeking Protection from Evil: "Aʿūdhu bika min sharri mā ṣanaʿt"

Balance of Gratitude and Humility

• Acknowledging Allah's countless blessings upon us

• Confessing our sins and shortcomings honestly

• Perfect balance between hope and fear

Exclusive Divine Attribute

• "Lā yaghfiru-dh-dhunūba illā ant" - Only Allah can forgive sins

• Recognizes Allah's unique ability to cleanse hearts

• Shows complete dependence on Allah's mercy

Gateway to Paradise

• Guaranteed entry to Paradise if recited with firm faith

• Should be recited morning and evening daily

• Contains complete submission and perfect repentance

Simple and Powerful Istighfar

أَسْتَغْفِرُ اللَّهَ الْعَظِيمَ الَّذِي لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ الْحَيَّ الْقَيُّومَ وَأَتُوبُ إِلَيْهِ

(Anytime, especially 100x daily)

I seek forgiveness from Allah, the Magnificent, Who there is no deity except Him, the Ever-Living, the Self-Sustaining, and I repent to Him.

Astaghfiru-llāha-l-ʿAẓīm, alladhī lā ilāha illā Huwa, al-Ḥayya-l-Qayyūm, wa atūbu ilayh.

Forgiveness Even for Major Sins

It is reported that whoever says this istighfar three times, Allah will forgive him even if he fled from the battlefield (a major sin in Islam). This shows the incredible power of sincere repentance combined with Allah's beautiful Names.

The Prophet's Daily Practice

Abū Hurayrah (raḍiy Allāhu ʿanhu) reported: "I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ saying: 'By Allah, I seek Allah's forgiveness and repent to Him more than seventy times daily.'" (Bukhārī 6307)

Gateway to Relief

The Prophet ﷺ said: "Whoever consistently seeks forgiveness, Allah will grant him relief from every distress, a way out from every difficulty, and provide for him from where he does not expect." (Abū Dāwūd, Ibn Mājah)

Invoking Allah's Greatest Names

• Al-ʿAẓīm: The Magnificent, showing Allah's grandeur and majesty

• Al-Ḥayy: The Ever-Living, acknowledging Allah's perfect and eternal life

• Al-Qayyūm: The Self-Sustaining, recognizing Allah's independence and His sustenance of all creation

Complete Monotheism

• "Lā ilāha illā Huwa" - Declaration of Allah's absolute oneness

• Purifies the istighfar from any trace of shirk

• Shows that only Allah can forgive sins

Active Repentance

• "Wa atūbu ilayh" - Not just seeking forgiveness but returning to Allah

• Tawbah implies regret, seeking forgiveness, and resolve not to repeat

• Shows commitment to improvement and righteousness

Practical Benefits

• Can be recited anytime and anywhere

• Brings immediate spiritual relief and peace

• Opens doors of sustenance and removes difficulties

• Recommended to recite 100 times daily
