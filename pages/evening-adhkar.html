Evening Adhkar

  1. Home
  2. Dhikr & Du'a
  3. Evening Adhkar

As the day comes to a close, we turn to Allah with gratitude and seek His protection through the night. The evening adhkar are a collection of powerful supplications that provide spiritual cleansing, protection, and peace before sleep.

These sacred remembrances help us reflect on the day that has passed, seek forgiveness for our shortcomings, and place our trust in Allah for the night ahead. They serve as a spiritual barrier against harm and a means of earning Allah's pleasure.

Reciting the evening adhkar regularly establishes a beautiful routine of remembrance, bringing tranquility to the heart and drawing us closer to our Creator as we prepare for rest.

Ayat al-Kursi: The Greatest Protection

اللَّهُ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ الْحَيُّ الْقَيُّومُ ۚ لَا تَأْخُذُهُ سِنَةٌ وَلَا نَوْمٌ ۚ لَهُ مَا فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَمَا فِي الْأَرْضِ ۗ مَنْ ذَا الَّذِي يَشْفَعُ عِنْدَهُ إِلَّا بِإِذْنِهِ ۚ يَعْلَمُ مَا بَيْنَ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَمَا خَلْفَهُمْ ۖ وَلَا يُحِيطُونَ بِشَيْءٍ مِنْ عِلْمِهِ إِلَّا بِمَا شَاءَ ۚ وَسِعَ كُرْسِيُّهُ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ ۖ وَلَا يَئُودُهُ حِفْظُهُمَا ۚ وَهُوَ الْعَلِيُّ الْعَظِيمُ

(1x)

Allah - there is no deity except Him, the Ever-Living, the Self-Sustaining. Neither drowsiness overtakes Him nor sleep. To Him belongs whatever is in the heavens and whatever is on the earth. Who is it that can intercede with Him except by His permission? He knows what is before them and what will be after them, and they encompass not a thing of His knowledge except for what He wills. His Kursi extends over the heavens and the earth, and their preservation tires Him not. And He is the Most High, the Most Great.

Allāhu lā ilāha illā Huwa, al-Ḥayyu-l-Qayyūm. Lā ta'khudhuhu sinatun wa lā nawm. Lahu mā fī-s-samāwāti wa mā fī-l-arḍ. Man dhā-lladhī yashfaʿu ʿindahu illā bi-idhnī. Yaʿlamu mā bayna aydīhim wa mā khalfahum, wa lā yuḥīṭūna bi-shay'in min ʿilmihi illā bi-mā shā'. Wasiʿa kursiyyuhu-s-samāwāti wa-l-arḍ, wa lā ya'ūduhu ḥifẓuhumā, wa Huwa-l-ʿAliyyu-l-ʿAẓīm.

Protection Through the Night

Abū Hurayrah (raḍiy Allāhu ʿanhu) narrated that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ appointed him to guard the zakāh of Ramaḍān. Someone came and started taking handfuls of the food. Abū Hurayrah caught him and said, "I will take you to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ." The thief said, "I am needy and have a family. I will not come back." So Abū Hurayrah let him go. In the morning, the Prophet ﷺ asked, "What did your prisoner do last night?" Abū Hurayrah told him what happened. The Prophet ﷺ said, "He told you the truth, although he is a liar."

This happened for three nights. On the third night, the thief said, "Let me go and I will teach you some words by which Allah will benefit you." When asked what they were, he said, "When you go to bed, recite Āyat al-Kursī from beginning to end. Then you will have a guardian from Allah with you, and no devil will approach you until morning." When Abū Hurayrah told the Prophet ﷺ, he said, "He told you the truth, although he is a liar. That was a devil." (Bukhārī 2311)

Evening Protection

The Prophet ﷺ said: "Reading Āyat al-Kursī from Sūrah al-Baqarah. If you read it in the morning, you will be protected from us (i.e. the devils) till the evening. And if you read it in the evening, you will be protected from us (i.e. the devils) till the morning." (Ḥākim)

The Greatest Verse

• Āyat al-Kursī is the greatest verse of the Qur'ān, describing Allah's perfect attributes

• "Al-Ḥayy" (Ever-Living) - Allah possesses perfect life, never touched by sleep or death

• "Al-Qayyūm" (Self-Sustaining) - Allah maintains all creation while needing no one

• His knowledge encompasses all - past, present, and future

• His Kursi (footstool) extends over all creation, showing His absolute dominion

Evening Benefits

• Provides complete protection from devils throughout the night

• Guarantees angelic protection until morning when recited before sleep

• Strengthens faith and trust in Allah's absolute power

• Brings peace and tranquility to the heart before rest

• Contains Allah's Greatest Names (al-Ḥayy, al-Qayyūm) for accepted du'as

Daily Practice

• Recite once in the evening for protection until morning

• Also recommended after every obligatory prayer

• Before sleeping for guaranteed angelic protection

• Reflect on Allah's greatness while reciting for maximum benefit

3 Quls: Be Sufficed in All Your Matters

بِسْمِ اللّٰهِ الرَّحْمٰنِ الرَّحِيْمِ. قُلْ هُوَ اللّٰهُ أَحَدٌ ، اَللّٰهُ الصَّمَدُ ، لَمْ يَلِدْ وَلَمْ يُوْلَدْ ، وَلَمْ يَكُنْ لَّهُ كُفُوًا أَحَدٌ.

بِسْمِ اللّٰهِ الرَّحْمٰنِ الرَّحِيْمِ. قُلْ أَعُوْذُ بِرَبِّ الْفَلَقِ ، مِنْ شَرِّ مَا خَلَقَ ، وَمِنْ شَرِّ غَاسِقٍ إِذَا وَقَبَ ، وَمِنْ شَرِّ النَّفَّاثَاتِ فِي الْعُقَدِ ، وَمِنْ شَرِّ حَاسِدٍ إِذَا حَسَدَ.

بِسْمِ اللّٰهِ الرَّحْمٰنِ الرَّحِيْمِ. قُلْ أَعُوْذُ بِرَبِّ النَّاسِ ، مَلِكِ النَّاسِ ، إِلٰهِ النَّاسِ ، مِنْ شَرِّ الْوَسْوَاسِ الْخَنَّاسِ ، اَلَّذِيْ يُوَسْوِسُ فِيْ صُدُوْرِ النَّاسِ ، مِنَ الْجِنَّةِ وَالنَّاسِ.

(3x)

In the name of Allah, the All-Merciful, the Very Merciful. Say, He is Allah, the One, the Self-Sufficient Master, Who has not given birth and was not born, and to Whom no one is equal. (112)

In the name of Allah, the All-Merciful, the Very Merciful. Say, I seek protection of the Lord of the daybreak, from the evil of what He has created, and from the evil of the darkening night when it settles, and from the evil of the blowers in knots, and from the evil of the envier when he envies. (113)

In the name of Allah, the All-Merciful, the Very Merciful. Say, I seek protection of the Lord of mankind, the King of mankind, the God of mankind, from the evil of the whisperer who withdraws, who whispers in the hearts of mankind, whether they be Jinn or people. (114)

Bismi-llāhi-r-Raḥmāni-r-Raḥīm. Qul Huwa-llāhu Aḥad. Allāhu-ṣ-Ṣamad. Lam yalid wa lam yūlad. Wa lam yakul-lahū kufuwan aḥad.

Bismi-llāhi-r-Raḥmāni-r-Raḥīm. Qul aʿūdhu bi-Rabbi-l-falaq. Min sharri mā khalaq. Wa min sharri ghāsiqin idhā waqab. Wa min sharri-n-naffāthāti fi-l-ʿuqad. Wa min sharri ḥāsidin idhā ḥasad.

Bismi-llāhi-r-Raḥmāni-r-Raḥīm. Qul aʿūdhu bi-Rabbi-n-nās. Maliki-n-nās. Ilāh-hin-nās. Min sharri-l-waswāsi-l-khannās. Al-ladhī yuwaswisu fī ṣudūri-n-nās. Mina-l-jinnati wa-n-nās.

Muʿādh b. ʿAbdullāh b. Khubaib, narrated from his father (raḍiy Allāhu 'anhu), who said: "We went out on a rainy and extremely dark night, looking for the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, so that he could lead us in Salāh." He said: "So I met him and he ﷺ said: 'Say' but I did not say anything. Then he ﷺ said: 'Say.' But I did not say anything. He ﷺ said: 'Say.' So I said: 'What should I say?' He ﷺ said: "Recite Sūrah al-Ikhlās and al-Mu'awwidhatayn (Sūrah al-Falaq and Sūrah al-Nās) three times in the morning and the evening. It will suffice you in all respects." (Tirmidhī 3575)

'It will suffice you in all respects' means it will protect you from all evil.

Evening Protection: These surahs provide comprehensive protection through the night, shielding from all forms of harm including devils, evil eye, envy, and whispers.

Surah al-Ikhlas (The Oneness)

• Describes Allah's perfect Oneness and uniqueness

• "As-Samad" - The Self-Sufficient Master upon whom all depend

• Negates any partners, parents, or children for Allah

• Worth 1/3 of the Qur'an in reward

Surah al-Falaq & al-Nas (The Two Refuges)

• Seek Allah's protection as Lord of daybreak and mankind

• Al-Falaq protects from: all evil, night darkness, magic, envy

• Al-Nas protects from: devil's whispers (jinn and human)

• Together provide complete protection from external and internal harms

Evening Benefits

• Complete protection through the night until morning

• Shields from devils, evil eye, magic, and harmful whispers

• Strengthens faith before sleep with Allah's Oneness

• Provides peace and security during vulnerable night hours

• Recite 3x for maximum protection as taught by the Prophet ﷺ

Sayyid al-Istighfar: The Best Way of Seeking Forgiveness

(سيد الاستغفار) اَللّٰهُمَّ أَنْتَ رَبِّيْ لَا إِلٰهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ ، خَلَقْتَنِيْ وَأَنَا عَبْدُكَ ، وَأَنَا عَلَىٰ عَهْدِكَ وَوَعْدِكَ مَا اسْتَطَعْتُ ، أَعُوْذُ بِكَ مِنْ شَرِّ مَا صَنَعْتُ ، أَبُوْءُ لَكَ بِنِعْمَتِكَ عَلَيَّ وَأَبُوْءُ لَكَ بِذَنْبِيْ ، فَاغْفِرْ لِيْ فَإِنَّهُ لَا يَغْفِرُ الذُّنُوْبَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ.

(1x)

O Allah, You are my Lord. There is no god worthy of worship except You. You have created me, and I am Your slave, and I am under Your covenant and pledge (to fulfil it) to the best of my ability. I seek Your protection from the evil that I have done. I acknowledge the favours that You have bestowed upon me, and I admit my sins. Forgive me, for none forgives sins but You.

Allāhumma Anta Rabbī, lā ilāha illā Ant, khalaqtanī wa ana ʿabduk, wa ana ʿalā ʿahdika wa waʿdika mā'staṭaʿt, aʿūdhu bika min sharri mā ṣanaʿt, abū'u laka bi niʿmatika ʿalayya wa abū'u laka bi-dhambī fa-ghfir lī fa-innahū lā yaghfiru-dh-dhunūba illā Ant.

Shaddād b. Aws (raḍiy Allāhu ʿanhu) narrates that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "The most superior manner of seeking forgiveness is that you say [the above]. Whoever says it during the day with firm belief in it and dies on the same day before the evening, he will be from the people of Paradise. And whoever says it during the night with firm belief in it and dies before the morning, he will be from the people of Paradise." (Bukhārī 6306)

Evening Benefit: Reciting this before sleep with sincere belief guarantees Paradise if one passes away during the night. It provides spiritual purification and divine forgiveness to end the day.

Perfect Evening Repentance

• Address Allah by His Greatest Name affirming His Oneness

• Acknowledge Allah as our Creator and our status as His slaves

• Renew our covenant to worship Allah alone to our best ability

• Seek refuge from the evil consequences of our daily actions

• Balance gratitude for Allah's blessings with admission of sins

Evening Reflection Benefits

• Daily spiritual reset and purification before sleep

• Guaranteed Paradise for sincere believers who pass away at night

• Teaches proper humility and gratitude at day's end

• Cleanses the heart from daily spiritual accumulation

• Strengthens the bond between servant and Master

Formula for Forgiveness

1. Affirm Allah's Oneness and Lordship

2. Acknowledge our servitude to Him

3. Renew our covenant of worship

4. Seek protection from sin's harm

5. Express gratitude for blessings

6. Admit sins and ask for forgiveness

Protect Yourself From Anxiety, Laziness, Debt etc

اَللّٰهُمَّ إِنِّيْ أَعُوْذُ بِكَ مِنَ الْهَمِّ وَالْحَزَنِ ، وَأَعُوْذُ بِكَ مِنَ الْعَجْزِ وَالْكَسَلِ، وَأَعُوْذُ بِكَ مِنَ الْجُبْنِ وَالْبُخْلِ ، وَأَعُوْذُ بِكَ مِنْ غَلَبَةِ الدَّيْنِ وَقَهْرِ الرِّجَالِ.

(1x)

O Allah, I seek Your protection from anxiety and grief. I seek Your protection from inability and laziness. I seek Your protection from cowardice and miserliness, and I seek Your protection from being overcome by debt and being overpowered by men.

Allāhumma innī aʿūdhu bika min-l-hammi wa-l-ḥazan, wa aʿūdhu bika min-l-ʿajzi wa-l-kasal, wa aʿūdhu bika min-l-jubni wa-l-bukhl, wa aʿūdhu bika min ghalabati-d-dayni wa qahri-r-rijāl.

Abū Saʿīd al-Khudrī (raḍiy Allāhu ʿanhu) narrated that one day, the Messenger of Allah ﷺ entered the masjid. He saw a man from the Anṣār called Abū Umāmah (raḍiy Allāhu ʿanhu). He ﷺ asked, "What is the matter? Why are you sitting in the mosque when it is not the time for prayer?" He replied, "Never-ending worries and debts, O Messenger of Allah." The Messenger of Allah ﷺ then asked, "Shall I not teach you words by which, when you say them, Allah will remove your worries, and settle your debts?" He replied, "Yes of course, O Messenger of Allah." He ﷺ said, "Say in the morning and evening [the above]." Abū Umāmah (raḍiy Allāhu ʿanhu) said, "Then I did that, and Allah removed my worries and settled my debts." (Abū Dāwūd 1555)

Evening Relief: This du'a is especially beneficial in the evening to release the day's accumulated stress, worries, and burdens before sleep, ensuring peaceful rest with Allah's protection.

Eight Evening Protections

• Anxiety (hamm): Worries about future events or uncertainties

• Grief (ḥuzn): Sadness about past events that have occurred

• Inability (ʿajz): Weakness preventing us from fulfilling duties

• Laziness (kasal): Lack of motivation despite having ability

• Cowardice (jubn): Fear preventing us from speaking truth

• Miserliness (bukhl): Stinginess preventing proper spending

• Overwhelming debt: Financial burden beyond control

• Being overpowered: Oppression or humiliation by others

Evening Benefits

• Release daily accumulated stress and mental burdens

• Seek Allah's protection for peaceful, worry-free sleep

• Address modern mental health challenges spiritually

• End the day with confidence in Allah's protection

• Prepare the heart for restful night without anxieties

• Transform evening worries into trust in Allah's care
