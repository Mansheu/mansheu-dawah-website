About This Printable

The Islamic Calendar 2025 merges the Hijri and Gregorian timelines so you can plan school terms, work leave, and worship milestones at a glance. Each month features a watercolor skyline inspired by a different Muslim-majority region, reminding us of the ummah's diversity.

Hijri dates follow global moonsighting estimates and highlight major events such as Ramadan, Eid al-Fitr, Eid al-Adha, Ashura, Mawlid, and the Day of Arafah. Moon phase icons and prayer-time template strips along the bottom leave room to pencil local times.

Use it as a desk calendar, laminate for the kitchen, or slide each month into a frame for rotating inspiration. A printer-friendly grayscale version is included for notice boards and bulk distribution.

What's Included

  • Cover page plus twelve monthly spreads with Hijri/Gregorian grids
  • Highlighted Islamic events with short explanations
  • Moon phase tracker and blank prayer time bar every month
  • Notes column for goals, birthdays, or khutbah reminders
  • Colour and grayscale files for versatile printing
  • Quick reference page listing 2025/1446-1447 milestones

Islamic Studies Class

Printing Tips

Calendar Printing Guide

  • Print single-sided on 160gsm cardstock for sturdy wall displays.
  • Use borderless printing if your printer supports it to maximise artwork.
  • Punch a top hole and hang with ribbon, or clip each page to a wooden hanger.
  • Laminate and use dry-erase markers to update prayer times monthly.
  • Switch to grayscale mode when printing in bulk for eco savings.

How to Use

  1. Download the PDF and print either the colour or grayscale file.
  2. Write important family occasions, school terms, and travel across both date columns.
  3. Highlight Islamic events so children can anticipate special days.
  4. Update the prayer time strip at the start of each month based on your local timetable.
  5. Review the quick-reference page quarterly to ensure zakat and Hajj planning stay on track.
