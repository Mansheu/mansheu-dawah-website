Qur'anic Duas

  1. Home
  2. Dhikr & Du'a
  3. Qur'anic Duas

The Quran contains numerous beautiful supplications (duas) that Allah has taught us through His final revelation. These duas carry immense spiritual power as they are the direct words of Allah, perfect in their eloquence and comprehensive in their meaning.

Many prophets' supplications are recorded in the Quran, showing us how the righteous servants of Allah turned to Him in various circumstances. These Quranic duas serve as perfect examples of how to communicate with our Creator.

Reciting these duas regularly connects us directly to the Quran, purifies our hearts, and provides us with comprehensive supplications that address all aspects of life - spiritual, physical, and emotional needs.

The Greatest Dua - Al-Fatiha

اهْدِنَا الصِّرَاطَ الْمُسْتَقِيمَ صِرَاطَ الَّذِينَ أَنْعَمْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ غَيْرِ الْمَغْضُوبِ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَا الضَّالِّينَ

(Al-Fatiha 1:6-7, recited in every prayer)

Guide us to the straight path - the path of those You have blessed, not of those who have incurred [Your] anger, nor of those who have gone astray.

Ihdinā aṣ-ṣirāṭa al-mustaqīm, ṣirāṭa alladhīna anʿamta ʿalayhim, ghayri al-maghḍūbi ʿalayhim wa lā aḍ-ḍāllīn

The Greatest Chapter

The Prophet ﷺ said: "By the One in Whose hand is my soul, nothing like it has been revealed in the Torah, the Gospel, the Psalms, or the Quran. It is the seven oft-recited verses and the great Quran that I have been given." (Bukhārī 5006)

Conversation with Allah

The Prophet ﷺ said that Allah says: "I have divided the prayer (Al-Fātiḥah) between Myself and My servant into two halves... When the servant says 'Guide us to the straight path...', Allah says: 'This is for My servant, and My servant will have what he asks for.'" (Muslim 395)

Comprehensive Supplication

This dua encompasses all human needs: guidance to the right path, following the example of the righteous, and protection from deviation. It is recited at least 17 times daily in the five obligatory prayers.

The Straight Path

• "Aṣ-ṣirāṭa al-mustaqīm" - The path of Islam, clear and direct to Allah

• The path of righteousness, truth, and divine guidance

• A way of life that leads to success in this world and the hereafter

Following the Blessed

• "Alladhīna anʿamta ʿalayhim" - Those whom Allah has favored

• Includes prophets, truthful ones, martyrs, and righteous people

• Asking to be among the successful believers throughout history

Avoiding Deviation

• "Maghḍūbi ʿalayhim" - Those who incurred Allah's anger (who knew the truth but rejected it)

• "Aḍ-ḍāllīn" - Those who went astray (who were misguided without knowledge)

• Protection from both willful rebellion and innocent misguidance

Daily Necessity

• Recited in every unit (rak'ah) of prayer

• Constant reminder of our need for Allah's guidance

• Perfect balance of praise, gratitude, and supplication

Dua for Knowledge

رَبِّ زِدْنِي عِلْمًا

(Ta-Ha 20:114)

My Lord, increase me in knowledge.

Rabbi zidnī ʿilman

Allah's Command to the Prophet ﷺ

This is the only thing Allah commanded His Prophet ﷺ to ask for increase in. While Allah told him to seek refuge from various things, the only thing He told him to ask for more of was knowledge.

The Merit of Knowledge

The Quran says: "And say: 'My Lord, increase me in knowledge.'" This shows that knowledge is the most valuable thing to seek increase in, as it benefits both this life and the hereafter.

Comprehensive Knowledge

The word "ʿilm" (knowledge) in Arabic encompasses all beneficial knowledge - religious, worldly, spiritual, and practical. This dua covers all types of beneficial learning and understanding.

Types of Knowledge

• Religious knowledge - understanding Islam, Quran, and Sunnah

• Worldly knowledge - beneficial sciences and skills

• Spiritual knowledge - understanding of Allah and spiritual realities

• Practical knowledge - wisdom in applying what we learn

Seeking Increase

• "Zidnī" - increase me, showing knowledge has no limits

• True scholars always feel they need to learn more

• Humility in recognizing our limited understanding

Addressing the Lord

• "Rabbi" - My Lord, showing personal relationship

• Acknowledging Allah as the source of all knowledge

• Seeking knowledge for Allah's pleasure

Practical Application

• Recite before studying or learning

• Ask for both religious and worldly beneficial knowledge

• Remember that true knowledge brings humility

Dua for Righteous Offspring

رَبِّ هَبْ لِي مِن لَّدُنْكَ ذُرِّيَّةً طَيِّبَةً ۖ إِنَّكَ سَمِيعُ الدُّعَاءِ

(Āl ʿImrān 3:38 - Dua of Zakariyya عليه السلام)

My Lord, grant me from Yourself righteous offspring. Indeed, You are the Hearer of supplication.

Rabbi hab lī min ladunka dhurriyyatan ṭayyibah, innaka samīʿu ad-duʿā'

Dua of Prophet Zakariyya عليه السلام

This is the supplication made by Prophet Zakariyya عليه السلام when he was old and wanted righteous children. Allah answered his prayer and blessed him with Prophet Yahya عليه السلام (John the Baptist).

Quality over Quantity

Notice that Zakariyya عليه السلام asked for "righteous" offspring (ṭayyibah), not just children. This teaches us to prioritize the spiritual quality and righteousness of our children over their number or worldly success.

Acknowledging Allah's Power

The phrase "min ladunka" (from Yourself) shows complete reliance on Allah's power and mercy, acknowledging that children are a gift from Allah, not a result of our efforts alone.

Complete Reliance on Allah

• "Min ladunka" - from Your presence, indicating divine gift

• Shows that children come from Allah's mercy, not human planning

• Acknowledges Allah's complete control over creation

Righteous Quality

• "Ṭayyibah" - righteous, pure, good in character and faith

• Emphasizes spiritual and moral quality over worldly success

• Shows the priority of raising God-conscious children

Confident in Allah's Response

• "Samīʿu ad-duʿā'" - the Hearer of supplication

• Expresses certainty that Allah hears and responds to prayers

• Shows proper etiquette of ending duas with Allah's attributes

Broader Application

• Can be used by those seeking children

• Parents can recite for their existing children's righteousness

• Teachers can use for their students' spiritual development

Comprehensive Dua for Both Worlds

رَبَّنَا آتِنَا فِي الدُّنْيَا حَسَنَةً وَفِي الْآخِرَةِ حَسَنَةً وَقِنَا عَذَابَ النَّارِ

(Al-Baqarah 2:201)

Our Lord, give us good in this world and good in the hereafter, and protect us from the punishment of the Fire.

Rabbanā ātinā fī ad-dunyā ḥasanatan wa fī al-ākhirati ḥasanatan wa qinā ʿadhāb an-nār

Most Comprehensive Dua

Anas ibn Mālik رضي الله عنه reported that the Prophet ﷺ used to say this dua most frequently. (Bukhārī 4522, Muslim 2690)

Perfect Balance

This dua perfectly balances our needs for this world and the hereafter, asking for good in both realms while seeking protection from the ultimate evil - the punishment of Hell.

Covers All Needs

The word "ḥasanah" (good) is comprehensive and includes all forms of goodness - health, wealth, family, faith, knowledge, righteous deeds, and Allah's pleasure.

Good in This World

• Health, righteous livelihood, beneficial knowledge

• Good family, righteous spouse and children

• Beneficial deeds and good reputation

• Peace, security, and contentment

Good in the Hereafter

• Paradise and its eternal bliss

• Allah's pleasure and forgiveness

• Seeing Allah's noble face

• Intercession of the Prophet ﷺ

Protection from Hell

• "Qinā" - protect us, shield us from harm

• Shows awareness that avoiding evil is as important as gaining good

• Acknowledges Hell as the ultimate thing to fear

Perfect Supplication

• Addresses both positive and negative aspects

• Balances worldly and spiritual needs

• Shows the Islamic middle path - not forsaking either world
